short report on the Chicago show just ended

by bungotakadasword

just back from the Chicago show and here's the personal
prospective.........
no Japanese dealers present due to the big holiday in Japan at the
same time, so next year the show may switch to a different date.
Just speculation , ok ?

Personally I did a lot of buy/sell/trade............
as usual Walberg brought along his boxes of goodies and emptied my
wallet. Among the new toys I bought from the Big Guy is a signed Goto
school single fuchi with a peacock motif which matches exactly my
other mumei fuchi...so now I am quite positive the other unsigned
piece is of the Goto school.

Did see a few " big money " changed hands so there's some " actions "
going on there .

As usual Jim gave an excellent presentation/discussion on what make
a good tsuba. I, being my usual self with the big mouth , actually
has the audacity to say a tsuba " turned me off "......with
strange/interesting result !! Jim did mention there's no right or
wrong answer regarding the tsuba so i figure why not......

Benson's tsuba price is very very reasonable at the show but too bad
I dropped all my $ at Hagrid's.

The best part of the show is still touching bases with old friends,
telling the same ole stale jokes and seeing everyone is still alive
and kicking........
Perfect weather throughout and to cap the day, i get to spot the
resident red tail hawk's nest and see the magificient bird flying and
doing well.
For more detail and insightful report............ that you have to
wait till Barry gets home.
