Endangered Japanese Stingrays?

by apedraza_93

Folks,

I recently bought a sword from a very reputable dealer (zero
complaints as regards the dealer) and then had somewhat of a bad
situation when it came to the shipping company. The sword was coming
from Canada, and the dealer used UPS Overnight Express and gave me
tracking info.

The sword was delayed for a couple of days at the Louisville UPS
Receiving Center, and according to their website, a "non-customs
agency" was delaying it. After another day, I was contacted by a UPS
representative. I'd been worried that suddenly swords were illegal
here in the US or something, but no.

Bizarre as it may sound, the problem was the same (wrapping). U.S.
Fish & Wildlife had decided they wanted to know exactly what species
of stingray had been used in making the wrapping!!! I explained to
the young lady that these wrappings had been done in Japan, sometimes
centuries back, and save DNA testing or a reliable time machine, no
one was going to get the exact species.

This is the first time I've ever heard of this happening, and the
dealer was equally surprised and perplexed.

In the end the UPS girl said she'd put in the commonest species in
the form, and hope for the best. A couple of days later it was
finally released, and the sword is now in my hands.

Again, I can't stress enough how good the dealer was throughout, I'll
let him identify himself if he wants to, but I wanted to send this
note out as a warning to anyone bringing swords into the U.S. through
UPS, especially if the shipment clears customs through Louisville.
UPS Louisville was recently declared a Wildlife Preservation Clearing
station, according to the UPS lady. She added that Fish & Wildlife is
going to take a higher interest in this, and since I doubt any civil
servant (always excepting any who might be on this list) have a clue
as to what a nihonto is, more problems are likely.

Anyone else seen anything like this?

De wa,

Andres
