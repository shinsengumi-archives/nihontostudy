polearm tsuba question

by rkgatteleport

hey guys,

Are there any other polearm lovers out there - check out this tsuba - it
supposedly came off a nagamaki:

http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/front_small.JPG
http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/front_small.JPG
http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/edge_small.JPG
(there are a few other closeups in the same directory, but I didn't
quite get a couple of 'em in focus - sorry about
presenting this upside down, but I really busy last night and didn't get
a chance to rotate the photos...).

The piece is 2.885" by 2.73" by.21" thick at the rim and ~.175 in the
center. The rim appears to be
silver, and the sukashi pieces appear to be filled with lead.

The piece appears to be pretty old (Jim pointed out that it looked like
there were a couple of layers of lacquer
on it), and the lead makes it really heavy for a tsuba of this size.

So, has anybody seen something like this on a "real" naginata/nagamaki
koshirae? any attempts at kantei, observations,
etc., would be greatly appreciated.

thanks,

rkg

Re: [nihontostudy] polearm tsuba question

by yamaimojg

The style of the carving and the two or more coats of lacquer look old to me. It's hard to see much of the character of the plate itself. Silver fukurin aren't common on older pieces, but this one doesn't look so new. There are some early tachi koshirae with silver fittings. The size and shape seem OK for mounting on a nagamaki, but I haven't seen many old nagamaki koshirae for comparison. The namako sukashi (now filled) make me wonder if it was previously mounted on a sword, and if so that would further confuse the time period. On the other hand I wonder if the sukashi was made with the intention of adding lead fillings to increase the mass of the guard (lead is about 1.46x as dense as iron).

Seeing it in person would help. Might still be a puzzler.

Jim
