John Yumoto entry in Haynes' Index of Fittings Artists

by yamaimojg

I just noticed this entry in Bob Haynes 3 volume set.

MASAYUKI
H 04943.0

F: Yumoto
N: John, Masa, Yu
W: San Mateo, California, United States
D: born January 30, 1916, died October 1, 1988
NTS: born in Fresno, California, the second son of Yohei and Koshio Yumoto. In 1919, at age 3, went to Okayama Japan with his parents, which was the native area of his family. Attended school in Okayama, where his high school teacher was Dr. Kazutaro Torigoye, who also gave him instruction in the area of the Japanese sword and fittings. He also had excellent instruction in the making of word fittings form several teachers in the Okayama area. In June 1935 he left Doshinsha Commercial College and returned to the United States. In 1958 he wrote The Samurai Sword: A Handbook, which is still in print. He made a number of sword fittings, in both soft metal and iron plate, often as gifts to his friends, and received awards for some of them in Japan. He was an instructor and guide for several Western students, in all areas of both blades and fittings, including this author.

SCE. R.E. Haynes collection, 1960

by gaigin2000

Hi Rich;

I tried looking up this man in Haynes books but there are a ton of smiths with the Masayoshi name and who signed in this style.

He lists one however who worked in Kanada in Edo about 1800, who worked in the Omori style like this tsuba.

He was from the Ito school, His name was Sadashichi. He was a student of Ito Masakata. He worked on shakudo plate tsuba, with all over undercut waves in the classic style and quality of the Omori school.

For reference he lists the Church collection 550 to 553.

I hope this helps.

John
