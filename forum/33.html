the front of the thicker two-piece outer cardboard box. (I don't know the

hard to spot. I'd guess that you could get it at Isshindo:

Jim, Bob, and all,

Since my name was mentioned, I'd better respond! For anyone that doesn't
know me - perhaps many of you since I'm usually pretty quiet on all the
lists I've been on since Robert Cole's first - I am a notorious
bibliomaniac, among other obsessions, mostly tsuba these days.

Unfortunately I don't know any special terminology for these
boxes-within-boxes except "slipcover" and "shipping box." When I do get
books that have the shipping box part (still on used or on new books) I
archive them in the attic since they tend to look shoddy on my shelves. It's
a very Asian thing, although I have seen them on high-end European art
books. Is Alan Bale on board here yet? He's the real pro on books.

For anyone who hasn't drifted off to sleep through this, what originally
came to my mind was "chitsu," which is the name of the very Japanese folding
book cover with little ivory (or plastic) tabs that slip into little pockets
on the spine. Love those.

Of course I can't spot my copy of "Studies in Kyo Goto" at the moment, wish
they had put the English on the spine, too. But I did find my copy of "Tsuba
no Bi" that was discussed on the other list recently. In fact, I'm pretty
sure I have *two* copies of that.

Bibliomania - a perplexing and tragic affliction.

Craig Harris

PS - Thanks Jim for giving us a sane refuge.
