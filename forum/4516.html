Hi all -

One of the now-famous "Chinese" Japanese swords came to me for opinion this week. Talking to the fellow prior to the visit, I felt he had to be describing a "ringer." You wouldn't believe what he all saw in that sword and how excited he was about this "ancient" sword! I did ask if he wanted "the truth" about if when he came over, and he said yes. It took a couple hours and some show-and-tell to convince him. (He swore some Japanese offered him $10K for this sword six months back!)

My question here today...this sword had the typical "etched" hada that you could see from across the room. Has anyone figured out if these swords are being actually "forged and acid etched" or if this so-called "hada" is applied and etched (probably similar to fake damascus)? I couldn't say one way or the other 100% but suspect it is "applied." The nakago looked raw and certainly not something that even a blacksmith would make. Odd kanji, etc., and unfinished.

Comments?

Ron

RE: [nihontostudy] Re: any comments about this sword?

by robsongord

Hi Ron,



More troubling to me than the background of this sword is the penchant for
many to get a little knowledge and then go crazy about something they have
bought, even to the point of making up stories about it. Ive had numerous
individuals come up and ask me what I think of the quality of utsuri in
their blade, the amount of kinsuji, or to comment on the beauty of the
chikei, when none of the features were present. Its as if learning jargon
is taking the place of actual study  hands on study with good swords that
actual have some of these features. Then, it would not be possible for there
to be a market for these Chinese swords.



I might add that I myself, and even Yoshikawa sensei, have been sent e-mails
or letters from Thailand, Indonesia and who knows where else asking us to
either buy or to comment on this garbage. In each case I have told the
individual exactly what they have, if they bought them, they should report
the seller for fraud, or if they are the seller, warning them that they can
be prosecuted for attempting to perpetrate a fraud. Unfortunately, people
are still being taken. I would hope that when Bob Benson or anyone else puts
on a display of top quality pieces somewhere in the States, everyone takes
the opportunity to go and learn.



Just my morning rant.



Gordon
