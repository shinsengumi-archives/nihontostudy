Another kodzuka ...

by drjohnplissenden

I have read somewhere - I cannot remember where - that Goto menuki
have sometimes been mounted as the decoration on kodzuka or kogai. Is
the kodzuka illustrated in my photo album JPL one such?

It is of good quality shakudo, with a worn, horizontal nanako surface
within a raised rim. The decoration is a raised shakudo boss with a
circumferential nanako decoration. This rests upon a gold base, and
depicts six pawlonia mon of the 3-5-3 variety, with a seventh set deep
at the centre. This one is less worn than the other six, and is very
detailed in its execution. The whole sits upon a silver ?shuttle.
The reverse is of polished shakudo, and the kodzuka is unsigned.

See http://tinyurl.com/77rxt

Regards and thanks, John L.

by robsongord

Hi John,

It is most likely not a single menuki, but it is an applied piece. The ruler
is the type used in calligraphy and is a common late Muromachi to Momoyama
period design. It is a rather nice piece. Goto kiri mon have identifying
punch marks, but your photos are too small to confirm them.

Gordon
