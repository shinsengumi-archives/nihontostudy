Re: [nihontostudy] Re: tsuba motifs

by yamaimojg

I agree with kiri leaf mon at about 1:00 and 7:00. Probably gourds at 3:00
and 9:00. The birds lack long necks, so they're probably not geese. The
things at ~5:00 and 11:00 look like folding fans as David said. Rather than
a koto bridge, I think that the curving elements are positive relief stems
for the negative relief gingko leaves formed next to the kiri/gourd. It
looks like the middle of the stem is missing from the left side, but the end
of the stem is there at the top.

I don't know what the combined meaning is. Both gourds and kiri were
associated with Hideyoshi. Gingko is a samurai loyalty symbol and is
associated with shrines and temples. They're also planted around the
Imperial palace grounds in Tokyo, although I don't know if they're old
enough to have been there when it was Tokugawa property. If the birds are
swallows that would go with the moat. I can't think of anything specific
about the fans. Anyway, just thinking out loud, I don't know what it adds
up to. Maybe some particular shrine or scenic spot? The gourds and fans
for an outdoor party held there?

Jim
