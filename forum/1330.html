by yamaimojg

Milt,
Tom is of course right about the motif being a shachihoko. The construction of a copper core sandwiched between thin gold layers and finished with a fukurin is not that unusual. It's seen in Momoyama period Ko-Kinko work for example, often with wave and floating mon motifs. Most of these are not as high relief as yours and are usually not so crisply carved. The shape of the hitsu-ana and the general workmanship of this one seem more in line with the Edo period. Of course the hitsu-ana could have been enlarged later and relined with gold.

The Genroku period was the next point in time when this kind of display was in style. My guess is that your guard is from that time It doesn't look very late to me. It could possibly be earlier than Genroku. Hard to say more without seeing it.
