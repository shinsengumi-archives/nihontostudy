Recently acquired blade.

by atarbaktar

Hello:

I recently acquired a sword blade ONLY at a yard sale. From what I can tell
it was made around 1943ish by Asano Kanasane I have attached a picture of what
I have found on reference. The Mei is to me an exact match of example "C".

There is a small chip on the Ho, that is almost not viewable but it is
"feelable" as well as several parry marks on the Mune. The very end of the Kissaki
(approx 1/16") is broken off.

From the sources I have found this blade was very probably a showato sword
made for the Japanese Army. There are no other mounts or anything.

Now I know this isn't a "real collectable" but for the "investment" I made I
feel that it would be worth a decent polish as I can see a great deal of
activity along the Hamon even through the very light oxidation on the blade.

I will send some photos once I get them taken (my photographic skills are
marginal at best) if there is any interest. My skills lay more in the
metalworking end of things...

SO if anyone on the list is interested in helping out an old hammerhead I
would appreciate it..

Kanesane swords do have a fairly good following for a showato in part thanks to the Fuller and Gregory book and the fact that most were water quenched. A broken tip unfortunately will require a foundation polish making it difficult to get away with anything short of a costly polish and a good polisher.
