Re: [nihontostudy] new release / Yamanaka NL

by yamaimojg

To clarify for those who haven't seen it, this is more than just a reprint of Albert Yamanaka's original work. Paul Allman took apart a set of the original newsletters and rearranged them according to a logical table of contents. The first two volumes are as easily accessed as the Nihonto Koza or similar encyclopedia.

Volume I covers Koto, arranged by the classical road system.

Volume II is Shinto and Shin-Shinto, followed by History of the Japanese Sword, Identification of Nihonto through Shape, and Definition of Nihonto and its Nomenclature.

Volume III includes Historical figures, Famous Swords in Japanese History, Kokuho, Missing Kokuho and Juyo Bijutsu and Kyoho Meibutsu Cho

Volume IV continues Kyoho Meibutsu Cho, several articles by others, the Tokugawa Jikki and an extensive compilation of Random Thoughts.

Some of the most interesting material is in later two volumes, but an index would greatly enhance their usability.

The Allman version came out over ten years ago, but was "off the market" until recently. I see that latest offering does not include binding of the four volumes, so be sure to get them into some sort of binding before the pages get mixed up.

Jim

by neobliviscar

Jim,

I took the tsuba to shinsa, but may have not submitted
it. I believe the owner made a substitution (he had
only so many slots and given me more items than he had
slots). Same owner as the Myochin.

The design is exactly the same as Rich's, but it did
not feel Akasaka. The mimi on Akasaka usually feels
more full, rounded. Then the actual plate on the tsuba
was ~perfectly~ flat and the sukashi so crisp in
execution that it felt almost machined precise in
execution.
I mentally ruled out Akasaka, and was curious to
see the shinsa results on it. The fact that I don't
would point towards either (a) early senility or (b)
that this was the one substituted out.

Jim, (sidenote) at the time I was surprised at the
Myochin call on the other tsuba. I would have thought
that it ended up in some group I was less familiar
with. Interesting tsuba.

Any preview on the Yagyu exhibit for those
international listeroos that won't get to see it?

Curran
