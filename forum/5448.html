Looking for help to ID a tsuba...

by darpa_ems

I've had this tsuba for well over 20 years, & have wondered what info
there might be on it... It's a rabbit & waves design, with no ID marks.
It's iron, about 8cm x 8.5cm x 4mm. I believe it's a early 20th
cent. repro, at least I kind of hope it is, as it's been on several of
my practice swords over the years... When I got it it had a nice solid
brown patina, but enough rust scaling to need cleaning with a wirewheel.

Pics are here-
http://ph.groups.yahoo.com/group/nihontostudy/photos/browse/e85a

Any thoughts would be greatly appreciated. Not looking to sell it,
just info on period, school, maker, etc... :)

Thanks!

A.W.

Re: [nihontostudy] Looking for help to ID a tsuba...

by retalt

Hi AW, welcome. Yes I think this is a Repo also, but maybe late 20th
century as a guess. It's a copy, I believe of a Kô Shôami tsuba that was
originally done in a soft metal (Yamagane from memory) in the Momoyama
period, or perhaps a bit earlier.

Anyway. as I said, I think this is a very modern repo, just my opinioon
nayway.

Cheers

Richard
