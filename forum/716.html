(OK, kidding aside, the scoop is that you're probably not going to find
an authentic one signed like this... there was a group in Saga who
signed like this claiming lineage to Kaneie, but I gather everyone
thought they were rather full of it for doing so. They signed in the
same manner, though they were in Hizen. These exist I think in a void
between gimei and the real deal. Maybe it's like some dude downtown
claiming Picasso was his great-great grandfather, so choosing to paint
"picasso" style and signing "picasso." While those are not technically
forgeries, they're also not the real thing, but they are those dude's
paintings. I have one of the Saga group pieces (as do probably many
people), and I also have one that would seem to be an homage piece, not
signed Kaneie but signed Yamashiro Kuni ... Masa[X] which is not quite
legible... I am sending it to Cary for research.... disclaimer: I know
less about tsuba than I do about swords, correction is gladly taken as
learning information, I am passing on what I have read/listened to
intently, if there are errors in that it is because I have inserted
them out of ignorance.)
