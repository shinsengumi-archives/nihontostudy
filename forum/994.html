Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Naoshi fuchi

by yamaimojg

It's a fuchi that's made from another fitting, in this case a kogai. It's the same as the usage of naoshi in kogai naoshi kozuka: a kozuka that is made out of a kogai. Or a naginata naoshi katana: a katana made from a naginata. Naoshi does literally mean repaired, but in this context maybe remade is closer to the idea. If a kogai was damaged, but the jita was still intact it could be remade into another useful fitting. I suppose that it might even be done if the kogai was fine but not needed for mounting. Probably a lot of naginata or nagamaki that were remade into katana and wakizashi didn't have anything wrong with them other than being old fashioned weapons that could be turned into something more useful through shortening and reshaping.

RE: [nihontostudy] Re: Naoshi fuchi

by robsongord

I can try. One thing to remember is that Ko-Mino kogai come in very odd
sizes, particularly very narrow ones. This kogai could not be made into a
kozuka because it was too narrow. Also, Ko-Mino pieces are carved directly
from the metal - no inlays. They did overlays, which do not pop out. The
mimikaki was cut off and joined to the base of the decorative panel - the
line is still there. The kogai was very early, and so much of the work is
indistinct. Still an interesting piece.



Gordon
