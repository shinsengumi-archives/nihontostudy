I would be interested in finding the definitive answer for the mass
produced swords thing.

From what I've read, the "bundled swords" didn't even have a hamon (in
some cases? Or in all cases?)

Given that you might destroy your poorly made sword, and a poorly made
sword is even less likely to survive the process, I would think you'd
just heat the whole thing, quench, sharpen, and toss in the bundle.

It doesn't take that long to heat harden the whole thing, plus will not
destroy the sword.

On the signature stuff... Bishu = worse than Bizen I understand to apply
only to Sue Bizen swords.

My *guess* would be that the amount of effort going into cutting the mei
indicates the care taken... Bishu Osafune Sukesada/Kiyomitsu is easier
to carve than Bizen Kuni Osafune Ju Sukesada/Kiyomitsu.

As well as no date...

But for these swords that seem to have reasonably good construction, are
signed Bishu Osafune XX, and have a hamon showing activity... is it fair
to call those mass produced swords?

After all, the workmanship is showing things not associated with mass
production. It's only the signature that is saying something. So is it a
"bad" rule of thumb to judge these exclusively based on signature?

Or are there just different levels to the mass-production. Maybe less
skilled people/apprentices are given the work of making the Bishu
Osafune stuff while the talented guys are taking their time with the
better items?
