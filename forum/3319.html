Kodzuka identification ...

by drjohnplissenden

Can any kind and knowledgeable member assist me in the identification
of the maker of the kodzuka illustrated in my photo album JPL?

The obverse is horizontal shakudo nanako, surrounded by a raised rim.
At the centre are depicted two horses in high relief, one of gold and
the other of shakudo. The reverse is of polished shakudo and,
unfortunately, the mei is too badly rubbed to be easily identified.
It consists of five characters, the fourth of which may be 'taka' and
the fifth probably a kao. The first two kangi are NOT Yanagawa!

I fear that the very unsatisfactory photograph is the best possible,
considering the state of the mei but, given a suggested maker, I may
be able to marry that name to what remainss of the kangi.

With thanks in anticipation, John L.

Hi Rich,

Yeah, the cat is out of the bag mug shot wise. The koshirae is next to
my silly self and you can pick out most of the tsuba. The menuki at the
bottom left are Ko-Kinko, late Muromachi and Goto Joshin, late
Muromachi. The colection of fittings, bottom right are all Ko-Kinko,
late Muromachi and Momoyama except the two kogai on the right and the
two kozuka below them. These are Goto Sojo, Ko-Goto, Goto Joshin and
Ko-Goto. You can see that everyone was having a great time.
