Darcy - an alert.

I'm commending your website to a wealthy man who wants to buy a "faultless
example" of a Japanese sword. He won't talk to me directly, only through an
arms dealer friend of his who has asked me to help out as he himself knows
nothing about Nihonto. I find this very unsatisfactory and have declined to
help out. However, I'm writing an email to the bloke I know who is the
friend of the wealthy man outlining my reasons for not engaging, pointing
out the difficulties involved, and recommending his friend/customer do his
own internet research.

So, I'm looking at your site to give a "pointer" example of a good sword to
buy with koshirae, and discovered this:

Ryumon Nobuyoshi
Kamakura Koto (ca. 1288), with koshirae
NBTHK Juyo Token, a particularly important piece.
$74,000 -consignment-

Trouble is, the link on the sword name Ryumon Nobuyoshi to more detail is:
http://www.nihonto.ca/taima/

which is of course the link to the Yamato Taima/Taema below. However, the
link on the blade itself is correct.
http://www.nihonto.ca/ryumon-nobuyoshi/
