Raimon

by retalt

This question would mostly be for Gordon and Jim as it relates
directly to a NTHK Kanteisho, but anyone else who see's fit to throw
in can do.

I have a kanteisho which describes the tsuba theme as Raimon. As I
understand it, in Japanese, Kaminari (雷) means thunder and/or
lightning depending on the situation. If we want to distinguish
thunder and lightning, we say Raimei (雷鳴) and Inaduma (稲妻)
respectively and Raiden (雷電) for thunderbolt.

But in case of Raimon (雷文), does it mean an old Chinese form or
pattern which seems to express lightning.

Cheers all

Rich
