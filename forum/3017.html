Tsuba Opinion

by retalt

All, I have had an iron tsuba for a while now I have been studying with
little success. It came out of Japan with the opinion that it was Sôten
Tadashige but I have not been able to get myself into that frame of mind
from the day I received it.

I have finally got some better images of the partial mei that exists and
I think now it is Umetada Shigemasa. And I think the mei partialy reads
Nishijin ? ( Ju ) Umeteda Shigemasa. also thought Shigeyoshi could
have been an outside chance but there is a tsuba in Tôsô Kodôgu Meiji
Taikai that whilst has the Yamashiro no Kuni version of the Shigemasa
mei, has work ( waves and the like) that looks surprisingly close to my
guard.

Here are some images of the tsuba, and some spec's. It is Wakizashi
sized, of average thickness and quite heavy, and appears to be very
dense iron.

Size 73mm x 69mm x 4mm
Weight 140 gms ( 4.2 oz )

The images are labelled as Sôten as when purchased, that's what I was
led to believe it was, and I never changed that.

http://www.nihontomessageboard.com/tsuba_collection/soten_tadashige.jpg

http://www.nihontomessageboard.com/tsuba_collection/soten_tadashige_mei.jpg

http://www.nihontomessageboard.com/tsuba_collection/umetada_shigemasa.jpg

cheers all

Rich
