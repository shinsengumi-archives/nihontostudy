Re: [nihontostudy] Re: This just in

by retalt

Hi Verne, I guess they ( the police/government ) need people to be
honest enough to come forward with any un registered swords. This does
not effect genuine collectors, though they will need to have a permit
and a Alarm on their house. Importing swords will be a different matter.
A license would have to be produced then I would assume. Also, I am in
Perth and visit Victoria as often as I can ( I am from there originally
) and taking any swords in with me will also be interesting.

My major concern is the amount of old WW2 swords that widows and the
like may have that are handed in to be destroyed. These swords are not
the problem, it is the junk you buy from sports and knive stores that
are the real problem.

There is more information coming in soon. I will forward what I get as I
get it, or Barry will.

Cheers

Rich Turner

by neobliviscar

Verne,

I cannot tell a lot from your sword photos. I wish
someone more experienced would step in and comment, as
I don't think I am very qualified with less than 5
years experience. Take my comments just as a beginning
point and allow for the fact that others may feel I am
absolutely wrong.

The kissaki looks chu to ko-kissaki and front part
tapers quickly. What is the length of the cutting
edge?
Tobiyaki (if correct term) is associated with
So-Den. My feeling off the sword is that it is Shinto
and hastily shortened for WW2 mounts. Out of polish,
unsigned, and just photos....very hard for me to tell.
I may be entirely misreading the blade, but am
operating under the belief that few if any WWII smiths
executed tobiyaki. I do not know which Shinto or
Shinshinto schools did.

Curran
