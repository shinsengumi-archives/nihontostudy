After WWII did the Allies make the Japanese register their
swords and were the swords stamped with a serial number after

Re: [nihontostudy] SERIAL NUMBERS ON JAPANESE SWORDS

by sworddad

Hello David -

Welcome and Happy 2006 to you too.

If you have a sword with a serial number stamped on it (on the blade) then you have a mass produced so-called "factory made" sword. Sometimes you will find some numbers stamped on the nakago (tang) of a sword, usually two or three digits, which in that case might relate to "assembly" numbers; these same numbers are often repeated on seppa, tsuba, etc. Swords were "registered" after the war and continue to be so today, but the Japanese government never required anything (registration numbers) to be stamped on swords.

Ron

Re: SERIAL NUMBERS ON JAPANESE SWORDS

by sigp210

I'd just like to add that the serial numbered swords are limited to
the NCO shin guntos with aluminium cast handle. I have seen many other
so-called Japanese swords with serial numbers on the blade, and these
were almost all Chinese fakes. For some reason, they think this
increases the value or legitimacy.
After the war, most of the civilian swords (bar a few identified as
art objects) were confiscated, not registered.
There is very little chance of tracking down the original owners or
history of 99% of these swords.

Regards,
Brian

Mark-
General MacArthurs' basement was located in Akabane a former
Imperial Japanese Armory taken over by US forces and one of several
spots where confiscated weapons were collected and warehoused. Over
the years several attempts were made by the Tokyo government to
return swords to their owners. Once they were sure there would be no
more claims made, the swords were examined, sorted and the average to
better ones restored. These were then returned to the prefectual
governments of their respective places of origin. Thus swords from
Sesshu went to Osaka and swords from Musashi stayed in Tokyo. Some of
them turn up on display in the local museums and there was a catalog
from one of the more recent exhibitions if I remember correctly. Look
for akabane-to...
