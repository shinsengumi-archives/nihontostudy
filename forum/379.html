Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Iron Tsuba?

by gaigin2000

Hey NC;

Well I for one would be one of the first investors in your patina paste.

The moist, wet, purple, feel and look on iron tsuba is a most desirable feature for collectors.

This condition I have only found on early iron tsuba forged from sand iron. The silky feel and texture of the iron I am quite certain is due to the quality of the iron plate and the method of forging.

The color I am not certain of.

Not all early tsuba even of good quality plate develop this purple color. Some tend to go towards black and some towards brown.

I know a tsuba collector who claims he can create this patina but the tsuba I have seen from him have fallen far short of the mark in my humble opinion.

Interesting question.

John
