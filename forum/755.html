Re: [nihontostudy] tsuba opinion, please

by yamaimojg

Milt,
I suspect that the missing body parts are meant to suggest that those parts are obscured as if behind clouds.

It may be the lighting, but the iron surface has the gray, shiny look of fire scale in places. Even with that uncertainty, the plate and the inlay don't look typical of Heianjo. The Jingo did make guards in that rounded square shape and did use a deeply hammered modulation of the plate. The condition may be confusing, but the apparent quality of the plate, the handling of the texture and the shape and placement of the ana don't come up to Jingo quality standards here. They did do some brass suemon zogan dragons, but the style is different from this one. The Higo Kinko Taikan has a lot of doubtful examples included, but if you can get a look at a copy, the Jingo section is worth comparing against your guard.

My guess is that it is some later worker trying to make something like a Jingo guard. The handling of the plate is consistent with Shoami work. The inlay is not exactly typical Shoami, but it's something that I'd imagine any number of groups could have done.

I think it's generally better to leave old lacquer alone, but with very rusted tsuba sometimes there is no way to keep it intact. If it is fire scale, you won't be able to bone it off.

Any other ideas?
Jim
