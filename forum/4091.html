Tokugawa tsuba

by steven_88_2000

Greetings all,

In my reading, I recall seeing somewhere that, early in the Tokugawa Period, the
government issued various "edicts"/measures aimed at standardizing aspects of the
wearing of swords (who may wear them, blade lengths, saya colors, etc...). I believe I also
remember reading that this extended all the way to the standardization of the hitsuana of
tsuba, among other things... I'm wondering whether any of you far more knowledgeable
than me might confirm this, and would also be able to supply an approximate date when
such edicts were handed down.

What I'm curious about is this: how possible might it be to (in part) determine whether or
not a particular tsuba was made in the early Tokugawa Period vs. the late Momoyama
based (in part) on the passing of this law? I'm thinking here that if a certain tsuba
possesses idiosyncratic hitsuana (or other non-standard features), would this allow a more
confident dating to an earlier time (again, as part of the assessment process)? Or are
there many variables (including lax enforcement of such "laws") which would render this
line of thought essentially useless? I think about such departures as Shimizu Jingo
(shodai) and think that maybe this isn't a very good barometer...! Anyway, just thinking
out loud here. Your thoughts appreciated...
