Re: [nihontostudy] Re: New Website for Japanese auctions

by retalt

Hi John, actually, 8% is not bad, I know of dealers and buyers in Japan
who charge 15% and get good custom. The 1000JY fee is for internal
shipping with in Japan. The issue is, many sellers in Japan won't ship
internationally. They don't want the bother, and they don't pack items
to ship out of the country. The intermediate buyer should handle all
that very well. Mine does.

Of course everything is a risk and dropping 10K on a sword in an online
auction is pretty risky anyway unless you really know what you are
doing. There's a lot to be said for holding something in the hand.

Rich

There's something interesting to this from a business standpoint. As
the buyer you have to deposit upfront the money through Paypal to
their account which they keep until the auction closes irreguardless
of whether you are the high bidder or not. Now think of this in
terms of if they get to be a big business. They could have quite a
large ammount of money on the kite -- making interest in their
account just like an insurance company does. And Paypal has a pretty
good interest percentage. Nice source of revenue! By the way -- they
aren't responsible for just about anything so read the fine print
carefully.
