Re: [nihontostudy] Hayashi Shigemitsu

by yamaimojg

Rich,
I only just got my issue. The comment about it being the 2nd outside of Japan is a reference to the October issue where there was another example published with the claim of it being believed to be the only signed example outside of Japan. How one would verify such a claim I don't know, but it's a bit hard to believe. Certainly attributed work is more common than signed.

Shigemitsu is the second generation of the Hayashi line after Matashichi, the founder. His work is well thought of, but is overshadowed by Matashichi, I think. You'll find examples in the Higo sections of Sasano's sukashi tsuba books, in general tsuba books or of course in ones focusing on Higo. Understanding the ins and outs of the Higo sukashi groups is challenging.

Jim
