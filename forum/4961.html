Yamon Naokatsu

by sworddad

Hi all -

Does anyone know of any connection that Yamon Naokatsu (2nd generation) had with the Tokugawa? Reason I ask, I have a short katana by Shoji Yamon Naokatsu (dated Meiji 4 - 1871) which has a silver habaki with the Tokugawa aoi crest on it in sukashi (pierced). I believe this habaki was original to the sword (not a collector's replacement) but so far I've yet to find some connection between the smith and the Tokugawa.

Thanks,

Yamon Naokatsu

by sworddad

Thanks everyone, for your imput about this mon habaki on my Yamon Naokatsu katana. Soon after I sent out this question it dawned on me that by Meiji 4 the Tokugawa were no longer in power and therefore, a meaningful connection between this sword and the Tokugawa would be unlikely. But I'm glad I asked since this did bring about some on-line discussion.

Interestingly, this sword which was owned for years by a good friend and eventually came to me last year, looks and feels much like a gendaito which I guess, it really is when you think about it. It is a very stoutly build and very masculine blade although made relatively short in length (63.7 cm). My other nidai Naokatsu which is dated 1865 is more a Nambokucho copy, measuring 77.9 cm with O-gissaki. I find it interesting to see the comparison in so few years but at a time of great "change" in Japan. It does surprise me that Naokatsu still had a patron at this time of change in power.

Ron
