Saotome women

by alchemyst

Thanks to the research by Jim and others, we have made
a major discovery in relation to the origins and makers of
Saotome tsuba, contrary to the standard lineage given in
Japanese sources.

Saotome tsuba are now known to have been made by
a group of women tsuba makers; the so-called "Rice
Planting Women". These were a group of women who
during the season were engaged in the planting of rice,
but who during the off season made tsuba as a means
of enhancing their incomes. These women tsuba makers
were excellent metal workers making well wrought tsuba
plates that they sold either as finished products or to
other schools of tsuba makers to embellish in that
schools particular style. This is perhaps the only
known instance where women composed a major
school of tsuba makers.

Thanks to Jim and others for their research in
bringing this important information to light.

Rich S
April 1, 2004 :-)
