Re: [nihontostudy] Kiyomune

by soshuju

Token Kantei Hiketsu doesn't say much about Kiyomune. On the Niô it
has this to say in part;

"Tachi no sugata haba semaku, kasane atsushi, (shodai Kiyozane) ooku
wa sugu-ha ni yakutari, ni dai-mei wa midare ha ni shite nie komaka
ni, boshi maruku, kaeri fukaku, hi o kakitaru mono ooshi. nakago wa
mijikaku, mune wa koniku ni shite ha no ho mo onaji, katayama gata
nari..."
Among the images in this section it shows a handrawn oshigata of
Kiyomune, and though it is far from photographic it shows that his
yasuri-mei was katte-sagari, the mune on his nakago was kaku and he
used kurijiri instead of the more characteristic katayama.
Tsunaishi in Nihonto no Kantei to Kansho breaks it down into Ko-Niô
and Sue Niô school and has a good two pages of info, however Kiyomune
is not mentioned and does not appear in his "family tree" for this
school.
I have added two scans to my new folder, Nihontoryu in the photos
section along with some photos of sutff I saw in Japan on a recent
trip but unrelated to this or any other thread...
-t

PS check the English edition of Token Bijutsu issues 13 and 42 for
info on Kiyozane
