Peter & Rich,

There are the 35 or so illustrated in the Owari
Tosan no Tsuba book. Pardon my Japanese. I'm sure
I just butchered the title.

I think there was discussion at one point of over 128
in print Yagyu designs, though the number may have
been higher. Jim Gilbert and Hans of the European
NBTHK society are the best to ask for the extended
list.

The rumor mill has made mention of a pending <2006 or
2007> Yagyu tsuba publication, and Rev. Kensho Furuya
made mention of a pending 2006? Yagyu book while he
was a member of the NMB. I am not sure this isn't the
same rumor from differnet angles, but I look forward
to any publication on Yagyu tsuba beyond the limits of
current reference. I greatly appreciated Jim G's
lecture in San Fran last year.

Re: [nihontostudy] Yagyu

by yamaimojg

Last year's Yagyu lecture and photos of the guards with the accompanying design book illustrations is scheduled to be ready for sale at this year's SF show as part of the regular publication of the sword lecture and display. This does not contain a complete version of the design book.

I have three different hand-copied versions of the Yagyu design book, two of which are just photocopies. I have the originals of Bob Haynes' own hand copy work from which I have made high resolution scans. I have Bob's permission to publish these, but haven't decided on print or electronic distribution.

I haven't heard anything recently about the rumored Furuya book.

Wishing the Americans a great Independence Day tomorrow.

Jim
