I have just received an email from a Oranda Token Kai member telling
me on the passing yesterday of Han Bing
Siong. He was well known for his pentrating erudite scholarly
works that reguarly appeared in the English and
Japanese language sword journals like Token Bijutsu, JSS/US and
Token Society of Great Britain.

I shall never forget how he answered every query I had however
insignificant and always had time for the younger
element. He once wrote, 'I don't feel its a waste of time to
answer any thing you ahve to write Yahya for you are next

RIP good friend

Re: [nihontostudy] Han Bing Siong passed away

by Arnold Frenzel

Friends:
I have been away for a few days and seeing this message today has come
as a shock and with it a real feeling of loss. While we have never met in
person, I corresponded with Mr. Han for many years and always found his
letters full of scholarship and interesting insights. While there are now
more and more well informed and scholarly people outside Japan, I
believe Mr. Han was without peer and his passing will leave a void that
will not be filled in the near future. His knowledge and analytical mind
has been noted widely inside Japan and out, and the sword community has
lost a true friend. I understand that his latest work, a compendium of
swords found and recorded in the Netherlands and rendered in English, is
soon to leave the printer. I am sure it will stand as a lasting monument
to his scholarship. I extend my prayerful sympathy to all of those who
knew him personally.

Arnold Frenzel
afrenzel@...
