Akasaka Tsuba?

by tsubahunter

I know that there are some knowledgeable tsuba collectors on this list, and I
would like your opinions and thoughts good, bad or indifferent. I recently
purchased this tsuba on eBay.

Sorry I don't have better pictures. I don't have a digital camera. These
are the pictures I grabbed off eBay before they disappeared. The pictures are
in my folder "Lou's Stuff." The piece looks better in person.

I like many collectors am still learning. I was wondering if anyone out
there is knowledgeable enough to attribute this piece to one of the smiths of the
Akasaka school. It is extremely thick. Somewhere between 9 and 9.5 mm. I
don't have any calipers at home so that is the best I can do. It is 81 X 79 mm
in diameter. This extreme thickness seems quite strange. None of the Akasaka
tsuba in Sassano's book or Kremers' book are this thick. From my limited
knowledge I thought that earlier Akasaka pieces are very open like this one and
are influenced by the Owari style. I'm not sure if I would say that the theme
is Owari in style but I would say that the thickness is.

Thanks to all who contribute their thoughts to this thread.
