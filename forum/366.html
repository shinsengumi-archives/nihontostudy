Hi all -

I've copied all groups in hopes for reaching somebody with an answer...

A sword of mine has horimono on which I've just learned, reads: Inari Daimyo Jin, which I understand is the name of a shrine. Searching on google.com I find this shrine is in Kasama north of Tokyo.

My question is this. Having this name carved on the blade as it is, would this indicate the sword had some association with this shrine or perhaps that the Owner had some association with this shrine? I realize there might not be a positive answer to this question but I'd be interested to read your comments and opinions either way. (The other side of the sword has a particularly beautiful "dragon/ken" carved within a very wide, short bo hi. The dragon is noticably "tightly wrapped" around the ken, an obvious characteristic [style/representation/form?] I have yet to fine illustrated elsewhere.) The mumei, short (25") ubu katana was given 78 pts. and attributed to Bizen Heiuezaimon Tadamitsu, Eisho (ca. 1504) during the September 2001 shinsa hosted by Chris Bowen.

Comments?

All of the swords that were on display at one of the shrines I visited
had a dedication of some sort carved into the blade. Some were very
crude, most of them made me wince "why do this to a good blade!" It's
possible that some were well done but after having sword TLC hammered
into my head from day one, from my point of view I couldn't consider it
as anything other than vandalism (read: this is my bias, and I know it's
a bias :-).

It is possible that your sword was dedicated to the shrine in question
and then un-dedicated by a serviceman or something.
