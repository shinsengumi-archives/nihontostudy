Looking at an old hako-gaki by he late Dr. Torigoye, he used two seals; one seal is a square "name" seal and the other the round "has a bird on it" seal. Does anyone remember when he used these two seals? I seem to remember one being used in Japan, the other when writing hako-gaki outside of Japan, but I'm unsure of that memory.

I have a notation on the "square" sealed box, "Zaiya" and wonder if that's a rating level of his.

Ron,

As I recall, the round seal with the bird was used for hako-gaki written
outside Japan. I also remember someone explaining the little marks on the
right side of Dr. Torigoye's kao signifying his evaluation of quality or
value: one mark "1st class," 2 marks "2nd class."

Of course hako-gaki, like origami, are still just an opinion. I had a tsuba
with a hako-gaki by Dr. Torigoye attributing it to Dai-go-ro, with his "1st
class" notation on the kao. I sold it to someone who found it in a book
attributed to Akasaka, and the NBTHK gave it a paper as Akasaka.

So it goes.
