Yagyu guards

by retalt

All, in my few modest years of collecting, I can't ever remember seeing any Yagyu school tsuba for sale. I was thinking about this this morning, Are they quite rare ?, They appear in enough books and I really like there simplistic design. I cannot remember ever seeing them on any of the commercial sites, both Western and Japanese, I don't really expect to see any on Ebay or Bidders.com.

are they that rarely available or have I just missed seeing them.

Rich and all,

A long time ago Tsuruta put one up on the Aoi website. I jumped on it, and
got an email back confirming I had it. The next day I got an apologetic
email saying that when he went to the shop he found it had been sold to a
walk-in customer. So it goes.

A few years ago Andy Quirt had one that is in the Kremmers book, and I had
him send it to me on approval. In addition to the nosebleed price, it just
did not grab me, and I sent it back. Bob Haynes later shared with me the
fact that had Kremmers over cleaned all his guards, and that's what had
turned me off without knowing at the time that was the cause.

I suspect that Yagyu guards - maybe most really great guards - change hands
either at the big auction houses or privately.

Craig

Re: [nihontostudy] Yagyu guards

by ccikkyudo

Craig,
Yagyu guards are not that uncommon.
At the annual Dai Token Ichi there are
always a few to be seen. They are
briskly sold if rationally priced.

I have been collecting them for 25 years.
I probably have 15-20 at this time.

I sold the piece you referred to that is in the
Kremers book about 15 years ago to a European collector
that was subsequently offered by A. Quirt.

You were smart not to buy it.
The reason I sold it ($1,200) was because
it papered as Yagyu kodai (later generation).
I sold it as such.

That fact seems to have been missing from the
description in the Kremers book.

P.S. To date no Yagyu tsuba have been
designated as Juyo Tosogu.
