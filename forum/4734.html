Yari and Naginata

by apedraza_93

Nihonto People,

I haven't seen a whole lot of books on Yari and/or Naginata in
English, so even though it was a bit pricy, I purchased Roald and
Susan Knutsen's book (http://tinyurl.com/yxlhuj). I just finished it.
I am very impressed and sorry I did not purchase it earlier. The
concise yet informative text plus the wealth of drawings and plates
was well worth the price.

If there's anyone else out there interested in the subject and that
has been on the fence about this great book because of the price tag,
well, stop considering it and buy it. Give it to yourself for
Christmas. You won't regret it.

And if Roald and Susan subscribe to these lists (or someone who knows
them does) please receive a big thank you from me... This book will
be a permanent part of my reference library.

Anyone have any other books on Yari and/or Naginata they want to
recommend? I'm thirsty for more knowledge now.

Thanks!

Andres

Re: Yari and Naginata

by excelsiorilluminati

Hi Mark, Pole arms have always been synonymous with defense against
cavalry throughout history and throughout the world. It is amazing
how similar weapons and strategies develop as a result of similar
necessities. Anyhow, with the development of the teppo and the
greater use of ashigaru in melee use of the pole arm declined,
however it never disappeared as a strategic weapon against cavalry.
If I recall correctly even during the American civil war there were
troops of billmen to repulse cavalry attack. The spear in many
cultures was the primary weapon as was the yari on early
battlefields. Again surviving until the 19th century including for
example the various mounted lancers brigades. Polearms are a vital
part of Japanese history and can't be ignored, these books help. John
