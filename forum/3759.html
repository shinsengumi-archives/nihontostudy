Re: [nihontostudy] Q:About Gunto mounting whith "signs" on all of the parts.

by Barry Thomas

G'day Patrik,

Believe me mate, yours IS a real Nihonto question IMHO. The "badge" on the top
of the handle fitting (kabutogane) is a mon - a family crest, and a very nicely
made one. These may or may not be real (in the "family" sense) but I won't talk
about that now. They are fairly often found and usually regarded as a "good
sign" of a better class blade in the mounting. Yours is one of the so-called
Genji mon, I believe, a symbol from history - perhaps someone knows more about
them. Hawley produced a sheet of all the symbols and I've probably still got
mine around somewhere (40 years of detrtitus hanging around my neck!!!)

The writing on the wooden part of the tsuka is I believe the signature of the
man who made the handle. Whilst unusual such signatures are sometimes found.
To me they indicate a custom-made tsuka by an artisan and not a regular "factory
job". Your signature has all the appearances of being "enhanced" by someone
with a felt pen, which is a real pity. Some of these signatures I have found
are of excellent calligraphy. I believe there were a few families of note who
were handle binders by profession and were involved in creating better class
gunto koshirae. Your tsuka is one of the best-looking and best condition tsuka
I have ever seen. Like new - the silk still has a sheen. You are fortunate, so
help to preserve it by only handling the tsuka with cotton gloves to avoid
soiling the silk.

The scratchy signatures on the metal parts are probably those of the owner and I
never pay them any attention as without other documentation it is near
impossible to find who the owner was..

The wooden blade indicates either (a) that a former owner polished the blade and
put it into shirasaya and had a tsunagi (wooden blade) made for the koshirae. I
have done this for a Tadahiro II katana I had polished. Or (b) that a previous
owner made his own tsunagi and simply sold off the blade...

Best regards,

Barry Thomas
(Melbourne, Australia)
