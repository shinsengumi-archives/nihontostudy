Richard,
Bob tends to date some things fairly early compared to common current (or past) Japanese standards. His approach is probably closest to Sasano's.

Just an anecdote that comes to mind... an experience Bob has mentioned a couple of times was a visit with Dr. Torigoye to a shrine in an area of Japan with a lot of old mound (Kofun) burials. In addition to the swords that had been donated, the shrine had a box of Kofun jidai fittings that had turned up over the years. Included among these was a kogai that was presumably contemporary with this material. This was at a time when kogai were considered to be a late invention. Today there are kogai that are accepted as Heian to Kamakura period. Dating of early fittings still needs a lot of work.
Jim
