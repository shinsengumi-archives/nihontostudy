Peter,
It's signed in katakana, which is a phonetic alphabet. It appears to read Haramu saku, with the ha, ra and mu written in kana and the saku with the usual kanji. The left stroke of ha is written a bit oddly, but I don't know what else it would be. The ra and mu are clear. There were some makers that signed in hiragana, but it is fairly unusual. I can't think of any old makers that signed in katakana. In modern usage katakana is used to write foreign words and names, among other things.
