Help with tsuba please - Yoshitame engraver?

by lshobie

Hi there,

I have a tsuba that I am trying to do some research on, it has an
elaborate grassscript type writing on it that I have had
translated. It turns out that it is a haiku peom written by
Baishitsu (1769-1852), and it means reads "Various impressions are
given to me every time the scenery of the season where the plum tree
blooms is seen".

There is also asignature which I am told is the engraver of the
haiku poem, "Yoshitame". Does anyone know who this person was or
what school he engraved for?

Does anyone have information on this tsuba?

Here is the link:

http://www.japaneseswordcollector.com/tsuba.jpg

Thanks in advance.

Regards,

Louis

Re: [nihontostudy] Help with tsuba please - Yoshitame engraver?

by yamaimojg

Louis,
Yoshitame looks like a correct reading, but sosho always leaves me wondering if there aren't other kanji that could be "abbreviated" in the same way. On the left side of the seppa dai there are faint kaisho style mei that might read Myochin. If that's what it is I suppose that it could be the plate maker.

There is a Yoshitame listed in Wakayama and Haynes. It's Haynes #H12160. Personal name Seiunsha [青雲舎], worked in Kanazawa in Kaga, ca. 1800, "made tsuba."

No mention of Myochin, but of course there were Myochin people making iron plate tsuba in Kaga at that time.

Jim
