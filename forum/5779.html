A non menuki question

by atarbaktar

Hello all!!

OK I have me a question...

I was working on a tachi out in the studio a little more than a week
ago.... got it all welded up in kobuse construction , shobu zukuri, and when I
was doing yaki-ire the dang thing curved like a cavalry sabre...I have
about 1 1/2" of sori on a nagasa of just under 28".. So how much sori is too
much??

I mean it is quite pleasing to the eye, very appealing in fact but it sorta
looks like a caricature of a sword.

The sori is tori-sori nice and even the length of the blade, so in keeping
with that I went ahead and curved the nakago to match the sori in the blade
to stay with the "lines" of the sword...

It is in mid polish right now and once I get it mounted I will post some
pics... I am amazed that the thing held together in the quench as it was
moaning and groaning and dancing around while it was in the tank but I didn't
hear one "Tink" or "Toink" and no cracks are coming up and I am at 800
grit on the polish (I don't use stones..I am doing this for book IV and am
using available abrasives that just about anyone can get...and I must say I am
getting some really nice results on the blades I am making...)

So did I make an abomination with 1 1 /2" of sori??? This should cut really
well with that much curve but I dunno...

Thanks all!!

Did you give it a reverse curve before yaki-ire? Or was it straight or

Matt:

It was pretty much straight..well as straight as I can get it with a
hammer doing the sunobe.

I think I may of done something when I changed the quenchant. Usually I
harden in hot salt water about 200 degrees F..This stuff I was using a mix of
sodium hydroxide, ammonium nitrate (Caustic blue salts) and table salt,
mixed in and heated to 290 degrees F.. I think that may of set it up for the
heavy sori.

As it is I am seeing a nice hamon starting to come up at 1000 grit..and I
am happy about that still no signs of any cracking...I did draw the whole
thing back to 450 F as a "safety measure" to prevent any chip-outs in
cutting. This was the first time I used this particular quenchant mix and temp
on yaki-ire... Kinda bugs me but it isn't that "bad" once you get use to use
to it...
