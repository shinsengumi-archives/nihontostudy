Another concept I just ran into was that of hot or cold shortening of
blades. If a blade is shortened "hot" the hamon will end where the tang
begins whereas if it is shortened "cold" the hamon will continue into
the nakago of the suriage or osuriage sword in question.

Now it never occored to me there would be two ways to shorten a blade,
although now ofcourse it looks quite obvious. However I am wondering,
presuming actually, that a smith would carry out the "hot" shortening,
not the cold shortening of a blade. I don't know why but that seems to
make more sense to me........

So who would shorten a blade "cold" and what advantages or disadvancages
would that have over shortening a blade "hot" ? Would it be possible to

the other ?
