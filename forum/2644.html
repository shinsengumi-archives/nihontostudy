We have the Art Institute if you are interested in Asian ceramics,
Japanese ukiyo-e.

The Field Museum has a large holding of tsuba but they do not display
them publicly.
We local yokels have a respected old timer that can arrange private
viewing . No, not me, I am not that respectable.
Other than that, there are a few collectors scattered allover the
landscape that may be willing to show you their stuffs but they have
to know you well first, I reckon. Personally. my sword collection is
low to mid range , no juyo. A lot of rusty tsuba I am working on but
I do say my hawk paintings are quite outstanding, 50 + now, not too
shabby.90+ % edo period, Soga school, Kano school, Tosa school, I
love them.

Milt
