Robert Haynes that a tsuba I had (I could not read the signature)was
made by Sukeyoshi. It is signed Sukeyoshi Saku. Robert told me he was
a swordsmith who made tsuba and signed his 'Suke' differently than his
swords.

Where can I read more about Sukeyoshi? Do you have any knowledge of
this smith?

by soshuju

Elliott et al -
That would be Yokoyama Sukeyoshi, who worked in Bizen and Fukuyama
Bingo on the cusp of the Modern era. His dated works are from Keio
(1865) and Meiji(1868). Sounds like a very cool tsuba, can't wait to
see the pictures. I have to admit, like Richard, I enjoy those late
Edo swordsmiths tsuba. I just bought a papered 11th generation
Yasutsugu tsuba at last weekends Taikai.
Addressing Richards question, the Yasutsugu tsuba I have seen are
rather plain, while Masahide seems to have put a lot of work into his
tsuba. Pure speculation but I would think he would have presented the
tsuba as a separate independant work. Who would want all that work
overshadowed by a blade or vice versa. I base it solely on my ill-
informed ideas of what a craftsman like Masahide was all about.
FWIW
-t
