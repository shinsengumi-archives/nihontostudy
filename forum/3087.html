Lot 829
"The design is no. 39 in the Yagyu family pattern book"



I've heard of its existence but never heard of it being published. Has anyone else?

by retalt

The Weight Bamboo guard.

Has obvious forging layers in the mimi ( quite flat ) 81 x 79 x 6mm, The
holes do not go all the way through. This looks older than all the late
Edo tsuba I have.

http://www.nihontomessageboard.com/personal/tsuba/weights-babmboo.jpg

Looks like long running bones but very very subtle. This looks mid/late
Edo I feel.

Middle timers story

by toyama1_2000

Well I got busy and forgot to add some sword stories
Since I am not an old timer nor new at it I gonna call
this "middle timers stories." Anyway, in 1990 Bob
Coleman arranged a sword appreciation tour. His wife,
Bill MIller, Paul Davidson, Mel Oka, Ray Deadman, Bob
Lewertt, Keith Hostler and myself went. Seems like
someone else but oh that memory button......Anyway too
much happened to repot it all but we did visit ( and
also dine with) Michihiro Tanabe. He laid out maybe 50
swords for us and we kanteied some. Kotetsu's , Kiya
Maro , freaking all the heavy hitters. The deadest
feeling block of metal was the Kiyo maru. Nothing to
it. We aslo went out for a really cool dinner served
by Geisha style chicks. Mr. Sugiyama (who was a
polishing agent for Tanabe) attended as well as Gordon
Robson. I have a back piece of a Samurai with a
naginata and made a fool of myself running around
wothout a shirt on. Jeez, no class. Years later when I
met with Tanabe at the NBTHK convention in Osaka (Paul
Davidson and I) Tanabe says "Oh you're the tattoo
guy!" Well I guess it's beter t be
remembered.....than........Bob Elder

If ignorance is bliss, how come I'm not ecstatic!?
