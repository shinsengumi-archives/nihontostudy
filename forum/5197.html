Re: [nihontostudy] How to call this odd one?

by soshuju

Yecheal -
Not sure how to address you, this is called a Shingen or Mukade
tsuba. Not so odd to those who have been around the study for awhile.
These are so called as it is said, but has not been proven, that
Takeda Shingen's hand-picked personal guard and messenger unit the
Mukade-Tai wore these on their swords. Shingen himself is said to
have awarded these as badges of merit to his troops. For this reason
they are quite popular and there have been many copies of this design.
Your example looks to be all copper given that the surface of the
seppa-dai appears to have been hammered and there are tiny stars
punched into the surface. Originals if such a thing exists are indeed
copper or brass wire woven thru an iron plate. Mukade BTW means
Centipede...
-t

It certainly means someone thought it was, but that doesn't mean much.
I have seen known repos go for alot. Most of us have (most of us
have also been the one to bid high as well at one point or another) I
don't follow that particular style so pricing isn't something I will
comment on. The one thing I am learning is that there were a lot of
"copies" made in the early 1800's, so they are old in one respect, but
they aren't the real deal. Then there all the copies made in the late
1800's that were more for the international/tourist markets. I don't
have a teacher and have been blowing my opportunities to be with other
collectors. I was so excited joining the NY token kai with access to
many knowledgeable people. But between my health issues and my kids
usually visiting me the weekend the club meets has put a huge damper
on me being able to go. I was picking up things here and there though
and it all goes back to the iron or base metals used. You start to
recognize the metal from the 1800's which is trying to imitate but
usually it is a bit overdone. I wish I could spend more time with
knowledgeable folks. I have to make my way back to the token kai.
Pricing by the way is something that I have struggled with since the
beginning. I think it just takes time to get an idea of where a piece
price range falls into. This is not an exact science by any means.
And unfortunately, the advice I was given on what it's worth hold
true, " it is worth whatever someone else is willing to pay for it".
Not a lot of practical help. So much too learn. Good luck and just
keep plugging at it. As Jim Gilbert says just keep on looking at
them, after feeling and studying over 10,000 you will start to get an
idea. Don't let that number discourage you, just keep at it. Best of
luck and remember the most important thing about collecting sword
fittings, Send all Praying Mantis themed fittings to me. LOL.
Thanks,
