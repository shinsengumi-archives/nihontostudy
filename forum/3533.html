Rich,
We have 6 local tails at the Chimney Rock site, including one distinctive female that has been seen for the last 16 years. They're common residents in NJ where they can also be seen every day. We only counted 117 high flying migrants on our ridge, but most of the northern population moves via the ridges to the west, where the season counts will number in the mid thousands. Your birds are probably year round residents like ours.
