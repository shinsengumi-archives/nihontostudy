NAKATA SUKEHORI GUNTO

by pixwords2002

hello -

I'm new to nihonto, so please be kind!

I've acquired an interesting shin gunto with the following traits:

- tang signed nakata sukehori, according to fred. weissberg
- black lacquered (not painted) wooden saya with field cover in fine pigskin; ink stamps
inside
- a fine mirror-polish blade with good, continuous ("active"?) hamon
- tsuba hole for the release button, but button missing; (the circular piece above the
grip is late-war and lacks this feature)
- grip has typical WWII furniture.

so some basic questions::

* what's the chance that this is the "old" goto sukehori and not nakata?
* how to distinguish machine-made from hand-made blade?
* why would sukehori sign a machine-made blade?
* with an 8-power loupe I detect no "grain." proof of a machine-made blade?
* the very edge of the blade seems to have sharpening (very thin - ca. 1/16"). Is this
as I hope the original edge?
* could the saya be an old one since it's lacquered?
* since the grip still has the sharkskin, safe to assume it's late but not VERY late war
(when fishskin was abandoned)?
* the field cover seems to have shrunk over the saya. I'd like to see the scabbard in its
glory. but - best leave alone?
* are the stamps in the saya cover more likely inspectors' or owner's?

many thanks for the patience and help with these queries!

I'll try to load a few pix....

Re: NAKATA SUKEHORI GUNTO

by excelsiorilluminati

Impossible to comment without good pictures of what you refer to. If
you see no grain in the hada it is certainly an indication it is
machine made, if the polish is good enough to tell. The hamon of a
machine made blade when oil quenched shows lack of activity, a clue.
Focus your pics on the nakago and mei for appraisal. Fred knows his
stuff so he is probably right. John
