tobiyaki

by davidzincavage

Benjamin Smith Lyman, writing in 1892, says:

"If the loam covering while moving the blade back and forth during the
heating, becomes thinner in some spots or loose, so that the heat of the
fire, and subsequently the chilling of the bath work more strongly, there
arise in the surface of the blade isolated cloudy spots called tobi-yaki or
yu-hashiri (sic). They cannot be produced on purpose, are rare, and are seen
with satisfaction."

My impression is that Lyman is mistaken, and that this feature can be
produced deliberately. Is that correct?

And are tobiyaki really regarded as especially rare and an unusual sign of
quality?
