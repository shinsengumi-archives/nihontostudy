While realising that the award of kanteisho certificates by the NTHK
or NBTHK for kodogu is an inexact science, I have a question to ask
concerning this.

Is the award of the various grades of such certificates based purely
upon the quality of the piece being adjudged, or do they take into
account the accepted reputation of the maker? Put another way, is it
reasonable to assume that all kodogu adjudged, for example, as
Tokubetsu Hozon might be of roughly the same quality,irrespective of
the maker, or might such an artefact by an artist considered as Meijin
possibly be superior to one, graded as Tokubetsu Kicho, by a lesser
artist?

Have I made clear that rather complicated question?
