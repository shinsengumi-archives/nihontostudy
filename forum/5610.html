The sound of a sword

by deanwayland

Good Afternoon Gentlemen,

This is going to sound like a somewhat unusual question, but it is one
that I am often asked, and am unable to answer, so I need some expert
input.

I have noted that symmetrically design swords such as some European
forms, "sing" with a clear ringing tone when impacted, even when done
gently, and that asymmetrical ones like reproduction Nihonto such as
Iaito do not. Instead these replicas produce a flat "clat" like note.

I have for some time presumed that this was due to their asymmetry,
however, could this be due to these weapons lack of hardness

Therefore can you clarify if Japanese swords do "sing" if struck?

Any help would be most warmly received.

With much thanks

Dean

by johnrbuk

Hi Dean

wakizashi blade with (kiku-mon) iga no kami fujiwara kinmichi signature removed from mounts, suspended on a rubber band, tapped gently on the tang with a coin emits a clear note which lasts for about 2 seconds that is the C above middle C ( checked against notes on a piano by my wife). The blade is not perfect - a few forging flaws - but probably dates from about 1650.

I could repeat this with some modern swords if you like but the iato you tried may not be steel

Hopes that this helps

John Bailey (in the UK)
