Nakirishi mei question

by alchemyst

The use of nakirishi mei is of course well known in Showa blades,
but I was wondering about it's use earlier in Koto, Shinto, etc
periods. It's been my (limited) experience that if a practice
is done in one time period, it's usually been done in another.
I would certainly suspect some of the "guild" schools like
Sukesada would have used it (pure speculation on my part).

Also, if it was a practice in earlier times, could this explain
why some blades get gimei at shinsa even though the blade itself
is a good example of the smiths work?
