Re: [nihontostudy] interesting tsuba

by yamaimojg

I think that what Rich called the "rail/nail" type is what's often called mukade, or centipede tsuba. The so-called Shingen guards are wire wrapped, sometimes so heavily that none of the underlying sukashi tsuba is visible. Others, like the one Peter posted, will have some of the guard covered with a brass plate and/or fukurin. I'm not aware of another term for this type of tsuba other than Shingen. However, there's no evidence that this style of tsuba had anything to do with Takeda Shingen, and if anything probably dates from the Bakumatsu period or later.

Rich, since you now have the 2nd ed. of Tsuba Kanshoki, flip to the end of the book to find a section titled "I will show here some poor samples as follows lest you should get such ones." The bottom of page 387 shows one of the full wrap "Shingen" tsuba and p. 389 has a mukade example. A lot of the old European collections had big, heavily wrapped Shingen tsuba. Whoever was making them seems to have created a market.

Jim

by soshuju

Rich et al-
It doesn't look that old to me either but I like it. I believe I read
in Fukunaga that Shingen's corps of top-notch troops was the
"Mukade-tai" , not sure of the tai bit, they were used as guard and
personal messenger for the commander. Besides the hata-jirushi (flag)
they were privileged to wear a mukade tsuba. My recollection is that it
is not certain what the actual design was but were held to be instantly
recognizable. Most examples are from much later times but there are one
or two of the "wired" design that have the right age but there is no
evidence linking them concretely to Shingen or this group.
I wrote this up for the NCJSC some years back so do not remember what
all it said or which issue it was in...
-t
