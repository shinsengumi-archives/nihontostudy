you all recall these menuki I took off from the tsuka of a sword i
bought on e-bay.

I found a very similar example from the Haynes catalog # 9, page152-
3, lot # 353.
I quote............ " highly important main line Goto menuki. The
plate is the very best deep black shakudo. The mochi cake is heavy
sheet silver uttori over the shakudo. The wiskers and eyes of the
rats are gold uttori. The hair of the rats is very well defined,
though worn down. The mochi cake has many of the secret main line
Goto marks. This pair would be about the time of Tokujo or Yeijo,
the fifth and sixth mainline Goto masters (ca.1575-1600 ). I have
seen another pair of menuki almost identical with this pair ( and
including the matching kogai ) with the original Goto papers stating
them to be the work of Tokujo. This pair is every bit as fine. Juyo
material. Ca.1600 "

Nothing is for sure till they are submitted for shinsa, at least it
looks " encouraging ".
