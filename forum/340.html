Hey guys,

After seeing Matt's message about the nebulous crane tsuba I realized I
had some good pics for comparison of
photographic techniques on tsuba...

These were either shot with my ancient acer scanner or my Canon G2
(color balance adusted by shooting a graycard).

This first picture is using my standard "shoot on glass over 18% gray
background with skimlight (and several white
cards used for additional lighting to the rim all the way around...)"
picture.

http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/kanayama_front_small.JPG

I had gotten a couple of complaints aobut how the rim was "washed out
(funny, I had started taking these this way
because that's how they were shown in Sasano's last book, but I
digress), so I reshot the tsuba using a samigon
fluorescent ring light on the G2

http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/kanayama_front_ring_small.JPG

The tsuba was laid on a graycard for this photo

Just out of curiosity, I also shot the piece using my scanner with the
lid open (the way I used to do this
before I got the G2):

http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/kanayama_front_scan_small.JPG

this probably could have benefitted from maybe a slight increase in
brightness (plus the resolution is less than optimal
due to my hurried reduction in size for this diatribe...)

The next picture is the tsuba with a white background (NOTE: I might
have increased the brightness slightly - I did this
a couple of weeks ago and didn't keep any notes - usually they look
about as bad as pics of the tsuba that Matt had
queried the list about):

http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/kanayama_front_scan_white_small.JPG

As you can see, the color is too dark, and you can't see much of the
surface detail (typical ebay picture, right? :-) )

This final picture is the same scanned picture with the brightness
cranked waaaay up (+43 or so in photoshop)

http://home.teleport.com/~rkg/misc/kanayama_front_scan_white_doinked.JPG

this was done on the higher resolution original - don't try this trick
at home on an ebay pic - it gets pretty
garly since nobody ever stores enough bits to do much with... In any
case, you can mostly see everything, and the
color is kind of correct....
