Hi David,

I am just back from my holiday. I went to the Sothebys sale, but as I
wanted to bid on one of the lots I had to miss the viewing. The sale was
well attended, with
quite a few Japanese dealers and I was told some from Taiwan as
well.There were I believe some Americans, Europeans and the usual
suspects from the UK.
Prices were mostly over estimate so no doubt Neil Davey who was
conducting the auction was well pleased. I left after some sixty lots
had passed because
I wanted to see the swords at Christies. I looked at about thirty five
of the best swords but after that I had to stop. My eyes could not take
in any more. You can only
really look at a small number at any one time. I came to the conclusion
that I would be happy to take home perhaps five of the swords, the rest,
some big names among
them, were not for me. You will know that at the sale prices went
through the roof, but I am still not convinced that a number of the
swords were correct. I was also
interested to see Caldwell's well known Ko Mino tsuba. It was much
better in the hand than in the pictures I have seen of it, and I liked
it very much. I have always
doubted though that it was Ko Mino, and as it sold for just over a
thousand I guess that the Japanese doubted it as well. Ko kinko perhaps?
My friend is still ill so
our discussions were limited but he thought that the kyo attribution of
our tsuba was more than likely correct. My own thoughts still linger on
Shoami but in the best
Japanese tradition I bow before the pronouncement of the Sensei.

Kind regards, Charles.
