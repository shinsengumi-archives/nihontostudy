Just For Fun

by nihontochicken

As we all know, the European and American Art Nouveau school of the
late nineteenth century was much based on the art disseminated from a
recently trade opened Japan. I collect old sterling and coin silver,
generally pre-1850, but am open to picking up a later piece now and
then if it has nice handwork (as opposed to the more typical punch
press garp). I just landed this small (5 inch) Victorian sugar sifter
ladle by Walter and John Barnard of London. The pierced and hand
engraved theme is ginko, I believe, very Japanese in flavor and not
typical of most occidental Art Nouveau (usually leaned more toward
acanthus). While not up to Japanese standards, the work here isn't
all that bad, and the piece was in my price range (cheep! cheep!
cheep!). Note that much of western-made Japanese-style Art Nouveau
metal work, particularly mixed metal, generally costs much more than
the equivalent type (and much superior quality) authentic Japanese
work (well, IMO).
