Pete and all,
I've been away for a bit (again), I'll work on catching up with the offline questions.

These are cut out for mounting on a koshirae with a spring retention clip. Not only are they seen on recent military mounts, but also on bakumatsu period toppei koshirae. In both cases, the single hanger style of wearing must have presented too many opportunities for the sword to tip out of the saya.

Interestingly, I've also seen this on older guards mounted on earlier koshirae, sometimes in Japanese museum collections. It's not at all clear if these were swapped-in tsuba, or if spring clip retention was also used at an earlier date for some reason. Perhaps for particularly unstable swordsmen.
