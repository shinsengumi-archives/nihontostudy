Just acquired this iron katana size tsuba.
Motif is that of rabbit with mallet set against a full moon.
It is signed Umetada Shichizaemon Tachibana ( ? ) Shigeyoshi saku.
a mid-edo period tsuba maker .
The flip side shows a crescent moon with waves.
Interesting thing is that the moon is overlay with VERY THIN silver
nunome now darken to same color as the iron plate. The " bumps " on
the moon surface, I believe, represents that mythical tree.

Along the rim are " chisel marks " ( can be seen in one of the
pic ), there are 6 on each side.
Question ........... are these marks for retention of a soft metal
rim now missing ? or are they " secret " identity marks of the tsuba
maker ?

Re: [nihontostudy] an interesting tsuba

by yamaimojg

Milt,
Nunome is often seen in Umetada work and indeed silver will become very dark with time.

The rabbit is associated with waves, pounding mochi (with that mallet) and the moon, as I'm sure you know. It's an interesting interlocking set of motifs.

I very much doubt that there was a fukurin or that the chiseling is a secret mark. I think it's just to add decorative interest to the rim. Worm eaten / rotten wood decorative devices like that were popular with the Umetada and Shoami as well as others to give an aged/rustic feeling.

Jim
