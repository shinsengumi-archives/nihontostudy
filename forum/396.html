Chikamura tatematsuru (saiba Juto)

by ccikkyudo

Alan,
You are correct.
I handled that sword about 10+ yrs. ago.
It was designated Juyo Token while in the possession
of a U.S. collector. The sayagaki by Homma sensei stated
explicitly that the sword was saiba but because of it's
extreme importance as reference material was granted Juyo status.
The only other extant example being in the
Imperial Household Collection (Gyobutsu).
It was sold to a Japanese dealer who understood its importance.
P.S. It was signed Chikamura Murakami.
The Murakami was not added later, but is read as tatematsuru.

[nihontostudy] Chikamura tatematsuru (saiba Juto)

by ccikkyudo

Alan,
Quite so.
If we are speaking about the same piece.
The one I bought and sold was designated
in Juyo Token session 26.
In the Juyo zufu index it includes saiba in parenthesis.
It was sanji mei: Chikamura Jo (agaru).
In this case the third character would be
construed to read tatematsuru (to offer
as a presentation to the Emperor).
I would have to recheck the Jufu page
to see if there is a mention of the
Mune character preexisting before Chika.
If so it would have read:
Munechika Murakami.
Nonetheless your explanation is rational
and is consistent with my memory.

The basic point of my post was that saiba
will pass Juyo providing it has a signature
which is of supreme historical significance
as reference material.
This qualified under the "special circumstances" criteria.
