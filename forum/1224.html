by samurai@xhasc.com

Complain using this form:

http://pages.ebay.com/help/policies/replica-counterfeit.html

Ebay will take them down in 24 hours.

There is only one reason that Ebay doesn't go after these guys in a much
harder fashion. They're accomplices to the sale. Every fake sold means
money in Ebay's pocket. If you turn on the lights while the thief and
his accomplice are robbing your house, the accomplice suddenly turns
into a vigilante and is glad to help you catch the thief...

That's the one and only reason Ebay doesn't do anything about it.

On the flip side of the coin...

There is a saying, "It's impossible to cheat an honest man."

Everyone on Ebay wants to get a steal. They want to buy your item for
less than it is worth, and if you ask a fair price few will go to
you. They will flock to those that appear to be offering discounted
items though, often times cutting deals outside of ebay, and all they do
is screw themselves. Their own greed means they end up with these
valueless pieces of junk.

Even armed with a little information, if they see someone who doesn't
appear to know what they're selling, how many of them send an email and
say, "Hey dude, that junky violin you found in your grandpa's attic is
really a Stradivarius. You should get it authenticated and sell it at
Sotheby's, pay off your mortgage and be able to send your kid to a good
university."

Very few.

So when you go out of your way to inform them, often times you take away
the mystery and the treasure hunting mentality takes them
elsewhere. Right into the hands of the scammers.

They want to buy something from you of extreme value, and not pay for
it. So they find someone who looks like they're providing this to them.

It's not only with swords, but it's with diamonds... other gemstones,
watches, everything. I sold some gold coins once, and I had no end of
people offering to do me a favor and give me 20% *less* than the market
price of gold if I would sell to them now and end the auction.

Gold is the one thing that has a well understood value in the world, and
even on ebay auctions will end at the market price of gold
consistently. The price of gold is less a reflection on the value of
gold then it is on the value of paper money.

These people were effectively offering me 80 cents if I would give them
a dollar bill. There is no mentality there for a fair deal, just a hope
that they can get something for less than it is valued.

For both reasons above, junk will continue to be put on ebay and the
auctions will be well attended. People will buy first and ask questions
later. And ebay will turn a blind eye to everything.

- darcy
