Interesting Sword

by dwmac2000

Hello Again,

I've managed to post a few photos in my album titled " Interesting
Sword." The pictures are mediocre at best, but will hopefully show
some of its charactoristics. The grain lines in the hada 2 photo
actually shine brightly as opposed to what is seen in the picture.
There is a bit of the hamon which can be seen in the upper section
of the hamon 1 photo. The sword is in obvious need of polish. It
laid on the battlefield at Okinawa during WWll for several days
before being discovered by a young Marine. It was stored away with
little or no attention since. Oddly, the sword seemed to have found
me as opposed to the other way around.

I'm trying to research the sword and have a little knowledge from
research I did on swords my Father brought home from WWll as a 11th
Airborne Paratrooper. I didn't realize what I was getting into!

I've had some much appreciated input from Ron and Ricky. I still
think judging from the Nakago condition the sword is most likely a
Bakumatsu era Ko-to copy. If anyone should happen to have opinion
as to time period or otherwise based on the photos it would be again
greatly appreciated!

Dave

Hello Dave,

Based on the images, it needs to be determined whether this sword has
been exposed to serious heat. The scaly-dry appearance of the steel
is an indication that it might have been. Fires were a problem
throughout Japan's history that affected many swords. There are
articles written that describe to some degree what characteristics to
look for.
In general, this is one lesson many beginners unfortunately
experience at one time or another.

regards,
Franco

Re: Interesting Sword

by dwmac2000

Franco,

Just an update as you were kind enough to offer advice and an opinion.
I reread a JSSUS article by T.C. Ford regarding fire damaged blades and
retempering. After reading this article and others, I can say with a
reasonable amount of confidence , Hamon loss due to fire and retempering
is not the case with this blade.

The blade is obviously not in top condition and I also adjusted the
photo scene balance in order to hopefully enhance the grain pattern.
The pictures do look as though the sword at some time had been recovered
from a fire. Particularly the Hada 2 and 3, but the sword really doesn't
appear that brown or scaly.

Due to the rough condition of the sword the Hamon is there, but not
that visible. I really feel as though the sword has not had polish
since the original. ( There doesn't seem to be any reduction from Nakago
to blade). I can also say the sword would look absolutely stunning with
a fresh polish. Future decision....

Thanks,

Dave
