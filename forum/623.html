by samurai@xhasc.com

Start here:

http://pages.ebay.com/help/index_popup.html?policies=replica-counterfeit.html

Go to the "Report" link at the bottom of the page.

Only the current "owner" can report it (if this is in the sold gallery,
you may be out of luck unless you want to fudge it a bit).

The above link is worthwhile to be on people's bookmarks. I often use it
to report fake swords, though that apparently does no good.

Going through there usually gets the sale removed in 24 hours.

You cannot email ebay staff.

- darcy

Remember that a fraudulent seller if on the way out has no problem at
all and nothing to lose with dinging you a negative.

Even if it is not legit, most people do not look deep enough for it to
register.

I have three negatives on my account, two from people who tried to scam
me on the buying side, and one because I left a neutral on a seller who
overclaimed the item he was selling. I got negatived for my honesty.

Anyway, the point is that it is not worth it to put yourself into a
position to have someone put a negative comment on your feedback.

Whomever wins should act sincerely. Petitioning ebay to get a bad
feedback item removed is a pain in the rear.

The auction will be promptly taken down if reported via the link I
sent. I have had someone take pictures of a ring I made and put it up on
ebay for $13,000.00 and it was taken down after 24 hours.

Re: EBAY FRAUD

by alchemyst

For those of you with expensive items on your webpages that
may be subject to this type of scam, you may want to
"watermark" your pictures, so the potentiall buyers can tell
what seller or site they are really from.

For an example see my top graphic of my opening page
(right at the bottom of the graphic).

http://www.geocities.com/alchemyst/nihonto.htm

This type of "watermark" is easily done by using the
"shadow" option on the script writer of any word
processor, just a matter of selecting the proper two
colors to be barely visible based on the color background
of your picture.

Rich S
