Re: [nihontostudy] A different view...

by soshuju

Jim-
We should be asking Mishina Sensei of course. If you look at
the Kantei Dokuhon (connoiseurs') you'll see the same style. I
believe it was Cary who pointed it out to me first, but his oshigata
are like negatives with black and white reversed. Usually the ji is
presented as white and the hamon is pencilled-in in black. This way
is "reversed" but also better approximates what the eye sees...

Re: [nihontostudy] A different view...

by samurai@xhasc.com

Nagayama sensei has a few oshigata like this in his book, and it helped
me notice some things in the second sword that I bought a couple years
ago that I would have missed otherwise. For a beginner, his oshigata
makes it absolutely clear what you are looking at and what you should be
looking for. A standard oshigata is also a good thing, but I think it is
more appropriate for well trained eyes who know what they are looking
at, and also know from experience what it would look like if they had
that particular sword in hand.

Anyway, having Nagayama sensei's oshigata coming up makes a good online
reference for smiths. It sounds like there may be more coming, and if
so, it's going to become a great quick reference guide.

- darcy

by soshuju

Peter-
I'll be corrected if I am wrong, but I am almost certain that
what you are seeing on the website are specific oshigata of
individual swords. In the Dokuhon, the oshigata especially for the
general characteristics of individual schools, the images are
"idealized" like in Teiryo Yoji. Sometimes I have found those to be
"not quite" what I am seeing in a sword. Takes some imagination to
reconcile what the book shows and what you see in hand. It is still
the best book for kantei.
These oshigata are REAL swords and it really would be
enlightening to compare, sword, photo, oshigata and "Diagrammatic
Oshigata" side by side...

Hi Jim and all,

I did not take a look at these until just now. I think that these are hybred
oshigata as only width and curvature is given, but not a specific length for
the blade. Also note in regard to the Kanabo information, no Yamato-den
style workmanship has been seen is false. I have a ko-wakizashi signed Nanto
ju Kanabo Hayatonosho Masazane on the omote and kore wo Hosho utsusu (this
is a Hosho copy) on the ura. The nakago has a ichimon style nakagojiri with
higaki yasuri like Hosho works and the jigane is masame with a narrow suguha
temper and nijuba, all typical of the Hosho school of the Yamato-den.
Remember, never say never.

Gordon
