I thought Mr. Haynes made some excellent points and organizational suggestions. He is certainly entirely correct about the Japanese sword hobby being dominated by a dealer's mentality. I personally am involved in a number of areas of avocational collecting, and I never seen another area of interest where so large a percentage of collectors are also dealers. An implicit paradigm of "find a sword, polish the sword, paper the sword, sell the sword to a Japanese buyer" lurks as a hidden goal assumption throughout the hobby.

I often marvel myself about the consequent universal American acceptance of Japanese preferences and tastes, for example the preference for owning a little-used ubu Koto sword over a well-worn suriage sword with a nakago full of holes testifying to considerable active use. The former may have simply sat in a temple for centuries, while the latter perhaps actively participated in some of the great events of Japanese history.
