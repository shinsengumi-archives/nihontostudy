<>This is just a note letting you know that I have a group of swords,
fittings, a tachi stand, and books for sale. Some of you might have
meet Dr. Louis Belschner a Dentist from Denver Colorado in the last few
years at one of the sword shows as he was selling off his collection. <>

Dr. Belschner passed away late last year and the family has ask me to
sell his remaining swords, tsuba and books. <>

Dr. Belschner was a good friend and I always had a good time when I
visited him in Denver. Over the last 25 years I have bought a number of
blades from him and he bought a number of blades from me. One of the
first blades I bought from him was a nice shin-shinto with dragon
horimono. At the time he wanted $500 cash for the blade. And I sure
wanted that blade. I had to call my wife and talk her into wiring me
the cash as I did not have that amount. It took all day but I walked
away with the blade very happy. The blade was in good polish to my very
beginning eyes but I could not understand why there were dips in the
blade between the large gunome. Turns out that he had used his dental
tools to clean up the blade and the softer metal above the hamon was
removed faster than the hardened edge. I wonder what happened to that
blade after I sold it? <>
