Re: [nihontostudy] a couple of tsuba to ponder

by davidzincavage

1) The leaves look like Aoi to me, and I think they are combined with a gourd which grows around to form the edge in a form reminiscent of Echisen Kinai work. I couldn't see a mei, and I do not find a Hisamasa in Haynes. I think it might be Bushu Ito. They did a lot of Aoi tsuba.

2) Dimensions might help. The corner elements, and the geese, are very abstract. I'd add Owari as a possibility.

Source: Wakayama, Toso Konko Jiten, p. 103, Lower page, 7th section.
