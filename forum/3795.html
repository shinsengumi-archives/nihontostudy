the tongue in cheek version of Tampa show report

by bungotakadasword

gangs,
Here's the " can you handle the truth " version of the Tampa show
..............

location...........great, no hassle about airport transportation etc.
etc.
but you all know that already.

Weather......... great the first day, second day it freaking rained
on us !!
It's fun to see all the old timers smoke chimneys huddled under the
stairwell
by the pool cracking old jokes.
Last day it's cold and the eastern seaboard states are all snowed
in............... have fun, boys from the east getting home on time.


events............. Jim's lecture and exhibition of Akasaka tsuba is
greaaatttttttttttt ( quoting Tony the tiger, god bless his soul ).The
NBTHK oversea branch exhitbition of swords and kantei ( open to all )
is excellent. I did not attempt the kantei as I don't want to
embarrass myself even though i would hit one right on ( the owner
told me his juyo Ko-Mihara would be there ). Then there's the
oshigata lesson, the demonstration of the clay thingie on swords (
hey, i am more of a fitting guy, can't recall the proper sword term
anymore ) etc......

Buying and selling................. buyers' market, no question
about it !!
If you carry cash and ready to part with them, great bargains
everywhere !!
Problem is most are freaking cheap................ or they all drop
their loads
by Hagrid's ( aka the big guy Mark walberg ) table who has boxes and
boxes ( I kid you
not ) of kodogu of the highest calibre ( and the price , though high,
is
reasonable ).
Mantis dude, i took a pic of a mantis tsuba from Hagrid's table just
for
you...............I think the asking price is in the mid 2k.
I personally marked my tsuba below retail and managed to sell only
two tsuba
in the higher mid range level and traded away a bunch of low end
tsuba for
somethings I can live with. So i consider the show as " slow " but I
do know
a friend sold a few high end tsuba so the show is good in his
prospective.

Foods................... the exhibitor's dinner on Saturday can only
be
described as " strange ".............
I wonder how our dear friends from Deustchland enjoyed the true "
southern
cooking " that include black eye peas und collar greens !!??

Jss/us business................ at least 10 new collectors signed up
as new
members at the show all due to the effort of one diector from Canada
( in
matters of hours ) !!
The society will be publishing Yoshikawa Sensei's writing (
translated by
Prof. Gordon Robson ) in 3 volumes all priced at cost !!
An excellent article by our John Berta on Onin tsuba will be
published in
the upcoming journal, on top of that out dear friend from Deustchland
Hans has
, most generously , gave us permission to reprint his article on Yagyu
tsuba. Considered what he ate on Saturday night, I wondered how he
can still
be
so nice..............may be it's because I personally asked him ? Or
may be he
had private dinner with carl who told me he had lobster with wine
sauce
prepared just for him at the hotel ?? Arrrrrrrrrrhhhhhhhhhh, life is
soooooooooooo unfair !!

that's about all I can think about just now.
For the " serious version " of the event, wait for the Ombudsman's
report.

p.s. also learned a new trick from one of the old timer...... when
checking Goto school kozuka, run your finger nail under the "
picture " side of the opening......if it catches, not a good sign.
