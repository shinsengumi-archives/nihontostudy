Re: Rich T Whats makes the front and back

by klepet33319

If you look closely to either side of the seppadai the small leaf and
branch have fine detail that make them the front side of each
accordingly. Also, in general the spacing to the left of the seppa
(kozuka) will be a bit larger than to the right (kogai) as would the
size of the hitsuana for them. These traits are all in the right
side photo.

Re: [nihontostudy] Goto Mitsufusa or Mitsutsuna

by yamaimojg

Richard,
It's Mitsukore. He was in the Shichirobei line. Kyo Goto no Kenkyu lists him as the 6th generation and Haynes lists him as the 7th. He was the son of Ryujo. He died September 7th, 1747. He also used the names Mitsusuke, Mitsunobu, Mitsutoshi and Joshin (different shin from the more famous Shirobei sandai).

Your signature shows some differences from the one shown in Kyo Goto no Kenkyu (p. 62), but that's the only example I have for comparison.
