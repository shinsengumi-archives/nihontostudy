Rich,
It's "Japanese Polearms" by Roald M. Knutsen. Published in London by Holland press in 1963. Hardcover, 271 pages. It is in English. It was/is a "standard" on the subject among US collectors, but it probably isn't that easy to find copies for sale these days. It's a rather impressive book for its day. It does not have oshigata or blade photos of the contemporary Japanese standard, but it has plenty of drawings and perfectly clear photographs of a wide variety of pole arms. It's more for studying the form of the blades rather than for looking at the details of hada and hamon in the individual items. There is also info on mountings and spear fencing, a list of smiths, photos of yanone, etc. I can't think of anything better in English today and as I said it's pretty remarkable that a non-Japanese had this level of technical knowledge of a somewhat obscure branch of the study only 5 years after Yumoto published his "The Samurai Sword."
Jim
