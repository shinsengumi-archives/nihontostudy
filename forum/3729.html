> In the book Suishinshi Masahide to sono ichimon, there is an
> oshigata of a similar tsuba and a few lines, explaining that he
> indeed did make more but not many. If I understood correctly all iron
> tosho style tsuba appropriately enough. I would love to see one in
> person myself.
> Your page says; This is a Tosho tsuba with kokuin impressed in a
> tsuchime-ji by Suishinshi Masahide. Masahides' great contribution to
> the world of the Japanese sword is evidenced by one well known fact,
> and that is that the Shinshinto era begins with the emergence of this
> very artist. His strength, his energy are evidenced by his having
> answered the demands of the times all the while fostering over 100
> students. He was an extraordinary educator.
> In Token Benkyo Kai first place is Ten (Heaven), second place is Chi
> (Earth) and third place is "Jin" (Man). With this work Masahide makes
> reference to that as well as Buddhistic themes. It is thought with
> this tsuba Masahide is showing us a miniature universe, his kokuin
> reading "Niten" is an abbreviation of Nitenji, a guardian deity who
> is the same as Nichirin (the orb of heaven, the sun).
> In Bunsei Gannen Masahide changed his name to Amahide, one story has
> it that the "Ama" was granted to him by lord Akimoto, suggesting a
> link to the first great swordsmith Amakuni and "lord of swordmakers
> heaven."
> The metal is a bit on the hard side but the color and texture are
> excellent, this is a rare and well known piece.
