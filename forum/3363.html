Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Yoshihiko

by yamaimojg

John,
The kanji for Saito inscribed on the guard Rich posted match the ones illustrated in the photos in Wakayama. The text version of those kanji in Wakayama match the ones in Haynes. The carved versions of the kanji do look quite different from the printed ones, hence your confusion.

The modern text version of the first kanji is so different from the carved one that I wouldn't recognize it on my own. The second kanji is a shorthand version of the printed example, but would be very difficult to recognize without textual context. I'd be interested in knowing the orthographic history of the first kanji -- it doesn't look like a post-war simplification. Does anyone know?

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Yoshihiko

by Barry Thomas

I do not think the first two characters are Sôshô - "grass" writing. I
immediately read the second one as -TO, as in GOTO (GÔTÔ????). Rather, I think
they are a cursive script that is a simplifcation of the Kaishô or "block" form.
Sôshô is a very much more "simplified" form and usually completely
unrecognisable without a lot of work (I think this is a "straight" comment and
hope I'm not talking with the forked tongue of an empty vessel!!

Regards,

Barry Thomas
