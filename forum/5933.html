Hello:

Ok my "area of expertise" is NOT exactly the Japanese sword..(although I
have made quite a few using the same methods, materials...etc for my
books...)

However there are numerous examples of various swords from the Bronze Age
that survive to this very day..so as far as what Mr Boyd says..I would TEND
to believe him at face value...however even the "best" of us can be mislead.

I CAN however "vouch" for the "Blood Rust" effect on blades as I have
seen it happen on various pieces I have restored..however the only pieces I
have seen it happen with are pieces from WWII and some from the "War to End
All Wars"..WWI.... Now I am not saying anything other than what I personally
have seen... now I do have some questions as far as this particular blade
goes..I find it interesting that in all the years and centuries past that no
one cleaned the blood off of said sword.. But this is "just me"...
