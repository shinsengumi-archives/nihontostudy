No, this is not a sword from the 12th century. Being a 2-character signature it is next to impossible to pin the fellow down, particularly with the blade in its present condition which looks pretty rough. A quick guess based on the condition of the nakago (on the photo) would make me think the sword came from late ko-to period or early shin-to which would be from the last quarter of the 16th century up through perhaps the first quarter of the 17th century. I don't recall how many Norimune are listed in that time-span in Hawley's, but you can check that out. Quite possibly a Bizen smith.

The Meikan lists at least fourteen smiths who called themselves
Norimune, Seven of them used the two character signature. Fortunately
the first five to use it are very early smiths, at least a very
famous guy, which leaves us with two later smiths both working around
Bunmei(1469-1487). One in Hizen and one in Bizen from the looks of
things Bizen may ultimately be the place...
