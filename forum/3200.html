by yamaimojg

David,
Look up ca. Momoyama tea ceramics in Raku, Shino, Oribe, Iga, Seto, Mino, Shigaraki, etc., styles. There's a lot more to tea taste than that, but it will be a start.

I think that your idea about debris might have come from terms like gomoku zogan used to describe late, low quality, "random" decoration sometimes retrofitted to older plates. I don't think that there was any intention to actually depict debris or puddles. Tea leaves -- no. That style of tea is actually a bright green, fine powder, btw.

I don't see anything Tenbo about the plate, but to your earlier question, there are some Tenbo guards with Yamashiro ju signatures, so presumably there was a relationship with Kyoto at some point. Who knows where the many poor quality mu-mei ones from later in the Edo period were made.

Jim
