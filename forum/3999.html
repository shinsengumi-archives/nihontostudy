my bid

by retalt

Nobuiye.

The hitsu ana threw me a little at first. I think nearly all the
documented Nobuiye tsuba I have have hitsu ana that are balanced in
size. The plate, the hammer work and the design all point to Nobuiye.
The large size also points I think to the earlier works, and not the
later Nobuiye's that worked in smaller sizes such as Akasaka Nobuiye and
the likes.

I would place this in the Muromachi/Momoyama period. Would assume it is
signed on the other side and think it to be a very highly regarded piece.
