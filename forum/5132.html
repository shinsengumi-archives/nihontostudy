match up well as authentic. However, with all of the repros being
produced I want to be as sure as I can before any purchase.

seen on the nakago. I have never seen a published example with so much
writing. It almost appears overdone on purpose so as to make the
piece look authentic.

by pbleed

Keith,
Interesting sword! I am not with my library at the moment, but this
certainly should meet anybody's definition of shin-gunto suitable for a
WWII display. Smith was a person named Katsumura Masakatsu. He appears to
have had/claimed a traditional title (hitatchi no suke??)and an honorary
affiliation (minamoto). The nengo is way cool. Looks like he made it in
1935 to commemorate the 2600 anniversary of the founding of the imperial
line - which did not occur until 1940. VERY patriotic. Way cool.
This sword might get more traction on the Token list.
Thanks for the posting. This looks like a very nice sword.
