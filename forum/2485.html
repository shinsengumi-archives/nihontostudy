masanori tsuba and fuchi

by samurai@xhasc.com

Hi everyone,

I'm a kodogu dummy. Anyone know what I'm dealing with here?

Signature looks like Masanori to me. Can someone identify who he is
exactly?

http://www.nihonto.ca/fittings.html

The material is silver. There is a kao on the tsuba and a signature on
the fuchi, and the styles do match. There is a kojiri on this saya as
well, along with a fuchi and koiguchi that are all done in the same
style. It's very nice, all pieces are silver.

Anyone have recommendations about the care and feeding of silver
fittings (Jim?). I'm guessing leave it alone and don't touch it.

The patina on silver is caused unfortunately by sulphur in the air,
which is a product of the industrial revolution. Black Tarnish (silver
sulfide) is not a native and normal patina for old silver in the days
when it was made... it's pretty uneven on the tsuba as can be seen, and
the kashira is also kind of rough.

I do want to know though if there is a proper care procedure if someone
does know. As well, I'd like to submit this koshirae for papers and
want to make sure I'm doing everything right.

- darcy

Dear Darcy

Yes - the signature on the fuchi is, indeed, Masanori. 'Kinko
Meikan' shows only one artist using these kanji (p.453, lower
right), but this is not the same artist. Haynes, in his 'Index of
Japanese Sword Fittings ...', lists no fewer than seven such
artists but does not show the kakihan (H 04383.0 - H 04389.0).

The seal on the tsuba I, personally, do not recognise.

So the fuchi is by a Masanori, but which one? If it is indeed part
of a set, the tsuba with its seal might possibly help in the
identification of the artist.

Regards, John L.

Darcy,
It looks like there was a storage problem with the tsuba or maybe a partial cleaning attempt in the past. It doesn't look like it will become uniform on its own over time (short of waiting for it to turn entirely black). I have no personal experience with repatinating silver, but I think that this may need professional work to restore an even patina.

I don't think that the present condition of the patina would be a killer at shinsa, but it would result in a "point" reduction. Polishing the fittings would definitely be worse than leaving them as-is.

Doubt I told you anything you didn't already know...
