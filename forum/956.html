Jim and All,
I got the magazine out last night and think it is interesting enough in the
history of sword collecting to recall to other. The article appears in the
SAGA, 1965, I think the August issue. The 'article' is really very short
and full of total hooey. Yamanaka is mentioned as the authority but not the
author. There are about a dozen nicely mounted swords shown
non-technically. These are noted as from the Ussui collection, but not
described at all. Then there is the list of 14 lost treasures. Each of
these gets a couple of paragraphs that list maker, date of recognition, and
date/place of surrender and in most cases the name of the American soldier
who received it. The source of this information is not explained.
My recollection is that the list that Albert presented in the Newsletter
was longer, but much less detailed in that it did not contain all the other
background information. I have never seen this list presented anywhere
else.
Peter
