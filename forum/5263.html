Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Help needed for identification ( new trial)

by yamaimojg

As far as I can recall, this style of work was first done in Kyoto by the Shoami. Certainly it was later adopted by many other groups around the country and some of those like Ito, Choshu and Kinai were bigger producers in the mid to late Edo period. Probably in some cases the work of those groups can be confused, but the iron, carving and inlay work tend to be a bit different.

Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Help needed for identification ( new trial)

by yamaimojg

Choshu and Bushu Ito used both nunome and "dot-type" inlay, as did Kyo-shoami.

Don't get too confused about Omori in this context -- they have nothing to do with iron sukashi. Certainly worth looking into as one of the major machibori kinko groups, though.
