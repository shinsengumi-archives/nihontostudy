sword found

by samurai@xhasc.com

Apologies for the bulk email (sent to the list and to some individuals)
as a lot of people were informed and I got a lot of nice supportive
emails from everyone who helped along the way.

This Yukimitsu was finally delivered to me after five weeks.

The final story as told to me by Canada Customs was that a postal
employee brought them the sword on January 24th (it was sent from Japan
on December 22nd by air), they then released it the same day.

The final story as told to me by Canada Post was that one of their
employees searched at Canada Customs and found the sword there.

Obviously not both can be true, so someone is covering their ass
somewhere.

Canada Customs was always very good on this whole thing were the ones
who gave me detailed information about Canada Post that CP didn't even
know about itself... information that was required to let me get the
sword in the end, rather than what Canada Post wanted to do which was to
write it off as permanently lost. It allowed me to get a message sent to
the warehouse manager that started the search that immediately turned up
the sword.

This identical problem happened to me one year ago in Montreal, with the
same situation... someone removed a sword from the stream, scanned that
it was sent to Customs, then it was put aside in someone's office where
at the end of three weeks of phone calls and searching it was later
"found" 15 minutes after I said I was sending the police.

Needless to say it is Fedex only for me here on in, and I could myself
lucky to have gotten both pieces back.

Thanks go to a lot of you who sent me supportive notes. I was pretty
deep in despair there due to the value and importance of the
piece. Those notes helped me make it through, so thanks.

- darcy
