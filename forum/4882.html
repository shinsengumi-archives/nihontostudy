����������������� Just thought I'd share this with you.  This is how things
go down in Canada.  I've copied and pasted the answer from my RCMP friend.
 
"So on to the legal question...in Canada you can use as much force as is
necessary to defend yourself and others but no more. In order words, you
must be able to justify the force you used. If burglar enters your home and
you surprise him (or her in a politically correct world) with sword in hand
and burglar flees...your done, the defence is complete. If, while burglar is
in the process of getting his/her butt out of your house you use your latest
iaido move to slice a pound of flesh off, you are in serious s**t.
Alternately, if burglar is armed and means to kill you for your meager
belongings and you use your best iaido move to slice a pound of flesh off,
then it becomes your word against his/hers. The most important
statement...you have to justify the force you used. The weapon you use is
irrelevant. You will have to explain why you used what you did and justify
it to satisfy a judge in court. THEN...there's the whole civil side of the
law. If, as a result of the pound of flesh you sliced off, burglar can no
longer function in society, can't hold a job and support him or herself
(even if that job is burglary) you might get your butt sued and will have to
support said burglar for a long time. In Canada it's all a matter of
justification of the force used."

Mark
