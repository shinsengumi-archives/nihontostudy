Seki Kanemasu

by sworddad

Hi all -

This is directed to anybody out there who might own a sword by Seki Kanemasu. Looking at Nihonto's site today I noticed Fred has a Kanemasu tanto for sale. I own two such tanto by Kanemasu, one having been featured as "Tanto of the Month" in Benson's Bushido publication years ago.

I find it quite curious that all of the Kanemasu tanto I've seen--my own and others illustrated--seem to have two mekugi-ana. One is into the Kane character and the other centered on the nakago above the mei. It makes me wonder if this was a happenchance of remounting or if perhaps it was some sort of "trademark" of this smith. I'd like to find out if any of you own a Kanemasu and if so, how does its nakago/menuki-ana look? Hopefully, one or two will be in your hands.

Ron

by robsongord

Hi Ron,



Such holes are not limited to Kanemasu, but are wide spread among Seki
blades. I think it is because the original hole was thought too low for Edo
period mounts. I think you would need to do a comparison of Tensho period
short sword mounts and later Edo period mounts to confirm that. I might add
that it is a common complaint among collectors of Seki works in Japan, and
also adds to the problem of identifying the distinctions among the
generations if you can't fully read the kane character. There does not seem
to be the same problem with katana. This would be a good subject for a paper
Ron.



Gordon
