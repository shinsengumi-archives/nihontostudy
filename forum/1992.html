Re: [nihontostudy] Kodogu kuestion

by yamaimojg

Tom,
I haven't had to deal with that. I've found that isopropyl alcohol is "patina safe" and good for removing some of the junk that winds up on tsuba. (Although if your tsuba has a patina "enhanced" by wax it will come off.) I don't know if it'll work on sumi. There is sumi ink remover for getting stains out of fibers:

http://www.shodokai.com/shop/product_info.php?cPath=33_36_46&products_id=110&PHPSESSID=5766575043bd7ac2cb2e9b6ce3d12734

I have no idea if it would work or if it would affect the patina. My guess is that it's an enzymatic stain remover like Nature's Miracle.

Sumi should be mostly carbon with some glue binder. Since there's nothing for it to soak into here it may just come off with water and some brushing.

Jim
