Ron & Glenn,

Ron- I'd agree with you that most tsuba just need
handling and appreciation past a certain point of rust
prevention.
Its been stated to me by 2 or 3 advanced collectors
that Sasano overpolished his tsuba. One fellow stated
that Sasano used 'a piece of carpet' and joked that he
couldn't help it- it had become a minor vice.

I am no where remotely near that point. By asking the
question publically, I thought it might be
interesting.
Once in a blue moon, a tsuba comes up that deserves
or demands that extra dimension of care. I'm currently
deal with one that merits it, and it is also an odd
shape that jeans care seems to do too much for the
mimi and not enough for the face. Wondering if I face
6 months to 1 year of flannel intensive care.

Glenn- "Hands on" is a must (eventually). All I read
about Yagyu tsuba could only take me so far until
Jim's lecture in San Fran. But until then- read till
your eyeballs pop out & learn many of the major kanji.
Still, under his q&a section on Jim G's website
there are pages upon pages of information.

Curran
