Large Tsuba

by davidzincavage

I recently purchased from Japan an unusually large tsuba. It is done in
both positive and negative sukashi, and inlaid with silver and brass. The
design features a Genji wheel and a thunderstorm. A lightning bolt strikes
the ground in negative sukashi, while brass lines of rain pour down beneath
clouds in negative sukashi and a positive silver crescent moon. The rim
shows thunder inlaid in brass.

My references do not discuss the combination of Genji wheel and
thunderstorm. Does anyone know anything about that motif combination?

I'm just starting to research this tsuba. Note the large size, almost 9 cm.
It came in an oversized tsuba box. It is surprisingly intact, but shows
wear. Opinions on school?

http://zincavage.org/WheelandLightningTsuba.html


David Zincavage

Re: [nihontostudy] Large Tsuba

by arnold frenzel

Hi:
You are showing the reverse. I do not know the "story" behind it, if there is one, but it is probably a early 19th century sort of revival piece based on earlier Heianjo-zogan types. I think it is rather too sophisticated for the more direct earlier material and it might be a combination of suemon inlay and nunome (the rain??? - can't tell from the image) in which case it would be late for sure. It could, assuming no nunome, be late Muromachi thru very early Edo too, based on the large size nakago ana, as large blades were carried then and in the 19th Cent. as well, but the design seems too cute. How thick is it?
AF
