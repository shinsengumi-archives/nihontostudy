Re: [nihontostudy] Animals on tsuba

by kanjincho

Matt,

Numata Kanji, _Tsuba kodo~gu gadai jiten_, 3 vols., 1967, would be the one.

This set was later reissued as a two-volume set in 1988. Here's a
description taken from an eBay listing of the two-vlume set:

""This is a dictionary of artistic theme on kodogu. Each kodogu (menuki,
fuchi, kashira, kozuka) and tsuba has an artistic theme (i.e., what it
tries to depict), and this "dictionary" attempts to list them all. Volume
I: Japanese and Chinese historical and legendary people, events, and
places. Volume II: things, animals, and plants. For each theme the
dictionary gives: a) the name by which the theme is known; b) detailed
description of the theme; and c) photo plate(s) of work(s) (mostly tsuba,
some kodogu) on which the theme is depicted.""

I don't have the set with me to see if they explain the "meaning" of each
animal, but I am sure the author touches upon it in the description.


Mike Y.

Re: [nihontostudy] Animals on tsuba

by musashibobenkei

Matt,

Tsuba kodogu gadai jiten, even though in Japanese, is not too hard to
navigate once you memorize Japanese "alphabetical" order (i.e. hiragana) as
the topics are laid out in that fashion. Plus it has decent pictures.

[Jim, is there any particular reason you have changed your originally
lukewarm opinion of Baird? ]

Also, if you can find it, Joly's "Legend" will be much more useful for
research with a copy of:

Tompkins, John B. and Dorothy C.
People, Places, and Things in Henry Joly's Legend In Japanese Art: An
Analytical Index.
Alexandria 1978, Kirin Books, 217p. Limited Edition of 2000

Expect a copy to set you back at least a couple of hundred $US. Not for
those that can't understand why the index can cost more than the book it
references (at least more than the reprint.)

Craig Harris
