Announcement: New Book on Hizento

by musashibobenkei

I am very pleased to annouce the availability of a landmark publication on
Hizento:

Yokoyama, Manabu. Hizento Biboroku [Hizen Sword Notebook]. Tokyo: 2006.
First edition. Manabu Yokoyama, considered by many to be a leading authority
in Japan on the swords of Hizen, has spent all his life on Hizento research,
and invested many years in the preparation of this book. A massive
Taikan-size volume of 547 pages with many oshigata and 10 color plates of
koshirae and old documents. All text in Japanese. Hard Cover with Slipcase.
New. $375.00

The book weighs in at over six pounds. I'll be shipping it Priority in the
US for $10 as it will fit in the USPS Flat Rate Priority box. Air shipping
to Canada will be $25 with insurance.

I will bring at least one copy to the San Francisco Token Kai for display,
where I will also have a selection from my other inventory. I look forward
to meeting friends old and new, stop by and say hello!

Also in stock, and worth mentioning:

Nakamura, Nick, editor. Kokusai Tosogu Kai, 2005 International Convention &
Exhibition. Tokyo: Kokusai Tosogu Kai, 2005. First edition. 108 p. The
catalog of the first Exhibition of Kokusai Tosogu Kai, an international
group dedicated to the study and appreciation of tosogu, or Japanese sword
fittings. Held in Louisville, Kentucky, the exhibition featured items from
Canadian and U.S., and Japanese collections. The Japanese items, most never
having been exhibited outside of Japan, were professionally photographed for
the catalog, and reproduced to the highest standards. The catalog also
features a 14 page article by Robert Haynes, "My First Sixty Years as a
Student of Kodogu," complemented by photos from his personal archives
documenting his years of study. Also included is an insightful colloquy
between Ms. Taeko Watanabe, president of the Sano Museum in Mishima, and
Terukata Kawabata, NBTHK Director and Director of Kokusai Budo Renme
(International Martial Arts Federation) on "Sukashi Tsuba, Their Use and
Aesthetic Beauty." A beautifully produced book and a rare treat for students
of Japanese sword fittings. Limited to 600 copies. Hard Cover with Slipcase.
New. $150.00

Along the same lines, I have recently obtained a few copies of:

Tanyu-kai Zuroku. Tokyo: Privately Printed, 1999. First edition. Tanyu-kai
is a private, by-invitation-only group of kodogu collectors in Japan. This
publication was done to record and showcase the best of their collections at
the time. While most of the fittings are soft metal, representing the many
schools of fittings makers, there are also a good selection of iron tsuba,
including a number of unusual high-quality Namban tsuba. 60 pages total, of
which 52 are full color very high quality photos of tsuba, menuki,
fuchi-kashira, kozuka, and kogai. Printed on very heavy paper and bound so
that each page spread lies flat. A beautiful and scarce book on which no
expense was spared. Hard Cover with Slipcase. Near Fine. $225.00
