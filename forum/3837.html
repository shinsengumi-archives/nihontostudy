Ron et al-
I think this is an (expletive) cool signature, looking at other
smiths working in Edo, Edo-jo does not seem to be used in any
signatures. According to the Meikan the first generation worked in
Edo but this particular signature (place name) is not given. So it
may be a unique signature.
Could he have forged this within the castle as diversion for his
lord or others? Could it have been for one of the sword-making
contests known to have taken place at the Shoguns behest. Was he on
duty (bivouac-ed) at the castle but forged this at the Kaga mansion
or some other spot in the castle-town? I think it raises some
intriguing questions and is a good subject of study.
As for preserving it, "every sword deserves a good polish", and if
they made it this far it is up to us to preserve them, if we cannot
get the best polish available then it is incumbent on us to stop any
deterioration, and keep it in as is condition for future generations
to restore.
MHO
-t
