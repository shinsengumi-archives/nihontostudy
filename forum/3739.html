by musashibobenkei

John,

Haynes lists two Ishiguro Masamori that signed with these kanji, H
04225.0 and H 04227.0 - the first said to be a student of the first
Ishiguro Masatsune (H 04751.0) but an independent artist, but the
name was also used by the second Ishiguro Masatsune H 04752.0. The
second Masamori listing appears to be for a student of the second
Ishiguro Mastasune who died ca. 1800.

The photos are a bit blurry - or maybe it's my eyes - and I'm no
authority on the signature. There should be examples in Wakayama that
someone can look up, my copies are in the warehouse.

by retalt

John, Craig and all. I had a look in Toso Kodogu Meiji Taikan and
Wakayama shows one example there. The kanji for both Guro and Mori are
quite different. The Guro is well separated in all the strokes on John's
example and the the published mei is quite compact and tight.. The mori
is also think and tight in Wakayama's but on John's piece, quite wide.
Also the Kao looks a bit different as well.

So, after all that, not sure. Some of these guys changed their mei as
they aged, or matured.

Cheers

Rich
