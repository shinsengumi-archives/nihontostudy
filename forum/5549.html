Rust does not come off with a cleaning. It need a polish done by
someone properly trained in that art, and with the proper supplies.
Attempting to do it yourself will ruin the sword and any value it may
have. Without looking at the sword, or at least a few good pictures
of it, no one can tell you what it is (Japanese or otherwise), nor
how old. With pictures some might venture a guess, but for a real
opinion you'll need to take it to someone who knows nihonto so they
can examine it.

Best,

Andres
