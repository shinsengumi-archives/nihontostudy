Re: [nihontostudy] Akasaka tsuba ?

by sworddad

Rich -

Interesting to see this tsuba. My Akasaka of this design signed Tadashige (to my relief) passed shinsa in Chicago. Of course I have it here in front of me now to compare the work with your tsuba. While the design is the same, the work on mine is more delicate (?)--more piercing--and seems to "flow" more, if you know what I mean. The horse's hoofs on your tsuba are more "squared" and "bold" than the more naturally shaped hoofs on my tsuba. I just saw a similar tsuba (picture) just the other day that showed these rather boldly made hoofs and it was called something other than Akasaka, but for the life of me I don't recall where I saw this. Thought it was in the new Mosle' catalogue, but if so, I'm not finding it now. My tsuba came out of a large collection of tsuba sold in Santiago, Chile in 2003. That collection also had another similar "horse" tsuba that had these large hoofs like your new tsuba, but I didn't buy that one. Most of these 730-plus tsuba were bought up by Michael Zomber, so perhaps your's could be the one I saw. I'll check my records.

Ron

Rich,
The appearance of the metal, the shape of the rim and your description of the lamination bones in the edge all point to Akasaka. The very small size and thin plate point toward later work. It's later than Ron's Tadashige. Bushu Ito people did use that design, but the metal and rim are different. The Mosle/Izzard Ito example is in shakudo and the carving is of course very different despite sharing the design.
