Recent acquisition baffles me

by grandone0

I recently acquired a katana that the seller swears is Japanese. He
says it is 130 to 140 yeears old. I've never seen one like it
before. It's saya is made of bamboo. The tsuba is gold or brass with
what look like narcissus flowers and pods. The blade is forged but
doesn't appear to be high-quality steel (hamon is midare). The
kojiri of the saya is flared. The kissaki is O and the mune is
ihori. The boshi is midare-komi on one side and almost Jizo on the
other. Nakago appears to be tanago. The ji has what looks like long
faint lotus flowers decorating it.

More strangely, there is no kashira, same, ito, menuki, on the tsuka,
which I believe is made of wood, rather than bamboo.

I ran the characters past a Korean and a Chinese woman and neither
could tell me anything. No surprise there, however I couldn't
identify the characters in either the Japanese or Chinese character
references on the Web.

If any of you fine scholars can tell me what Ive gotten myself into,
or where it might actually be from and when, I would appreciate it.
Please respond to the group and to my email address:
grandone@... I'm located in the USA.

Thanks so much for reading all this.

Regards,

Jim

really, really top class work. Way beyond my pocket...
