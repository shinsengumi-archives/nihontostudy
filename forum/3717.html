Kokusai Tosogu Kai Book and a few other books of interest

by musashibobenkei

I want to take a moment to wish all of you a healthy and
treasure-filled 2006, and bring to your attention a new publication of
interest to Kodogu collectors, and a few other books not quite so new.

Of the 600 copies of this book, 200 were reserved for Japan and sold
out. Most of the remainder were bought by the non-Japanese membership,
and some of you reading this might have received a copy as a gift over
the holidays. I have been given a small number to sell, and am
delighted to offer:

NAKAMURA, NICK, EDITOR. Kokusai Tosogu Kai, 2005 International
Convention & Exhibition. Tokyo: Kokusai Tosogu Kai, 2005. First
edition. 108 p. The catalog of the first Exhibition of Kokusai Tosogu
Kai, an international group dedicated to the study and appreciation of
tosogu, or Japanese sword fittings. Held in Louisville, Kentucky, at
the Frazier Historical Arms Museum, the exhibition featured items from
Canadian and U.S., and Japanese collections. The Japanese items, most
never having been exhibited outside of Japan, were professionally
photographed for the catalog, and reproduced to the highest standards.
The catalog also features a 14 page article by Robert Haynes, "My
First Sixty Years as a Student of Kodogu," complemented by photos from
his personal archives documenting his years of study. Also included is
an insightful colloquy between Ms. Taeko Watanabe, president of the
Sano Museum in Mishima, and Terukata Kawabata, NBTHK Director and
Director of Kokusai Budo Renme (International Martial Arts Federation)
on "Sukashi Tsuba, Their Use and Aesthetic Beauty." A beautifully
produced book and a rare treat for students of Japanese sword
fittings. Limited to 600 copies. Hard Cover with Slipcase.
New. $150.00

Not to ignore sword collectors, I have on hand a recent publication
that was mentioned on one of the lists not long ago:

KAWACHI, KUNIHIRA AND MANABE, MASAO. The Art of the Japanese Sword As
Taught by the Experts. Tokyo: Ribun, 2004. First English language
edition. ISBN: 4898062261. 156 p. This is a great little book, part
introduction for beginners, bits of history, a fabulous section of
pictures of every phase of the making of Nihonto. All of this from the
perspective of a working mukansa smith. The introductory sections will
be most useful to new collectors, but many things are pointed out that
are missing from the other classic introductory texts. Handy directory
to museums world-wide where Japanese swords can be seen. Glossy
Pictorial Cover. New. $65.00

And another excellent sword reference from the other side of the globe:

SIONG, HAN BING. Japanese Swords in Dutch Collections A Selection from
500 Descriptions in the Series Japanse Zwaarden in Nederland Bezit.
Rijswijk, Netherlands: The Netherlands Token Society, 2003. First
edition. 244 p. Translated from the Dutch by J. M. Bonsel and M.
Laros. This selection of 85 blades of the 500 cataloged by Han Bing
Song since 1979 was published to celebrate the 25th anniversary of the
Netherlands Token Society. The cataloging project included swords in
museums as well as private collections. Of those presented in this
book, a range of swords from Koto through Gendaito are shown with
oshigata and Mr. Han's scholarly and insightful comments. Seventeen
of the blades are also presented in photographs, and there are 14
pages of full color photographs of the best of the koshirae. An
outstanding publication of the highest quality and most valuable
content. Pictorial Hardcover. New. $125.00

Back to fittings, I have been thoroughly enjoying the next recently
published book. Mr. Ito was a student of Sasano, and I am told that
this volume is to be followed by another on Hirata Hikozo. I'm keeping
my fingers crossed!

ITO, MITSURO. Works of Kanshiro Nishigaki. Tokyo: 2005. First edition.
A lavishly produced survey of the works of the four generations of
Kanshiro Nishigaki, one of the four major Higo fittings makers. All
color photos, over 250 items, text in Japanese but photo captions also
in English. Hard Cover with Slipcase. New. $275.00

Finally, I have to mention a book that every student of the Arts of
Japan ought to have. This was very hard to find and quite expensive
for many years until the bulk of the 2000 copies, which had been
stored since the late '70s, were located a little over a year ago.
Anyone who has a copy of "Legend in Japanese Art" should have this,
and if you don't have a copy of Joly's book, I can help you with that
as well.

TOMPKINS, JOHN BARR, AND DOROTHY CAMPBELL TOMPKINS . People, Places
and Things in Henri Joly's Legend in Japanese Art An Analytical Index.
Alexandria VA: Kirin Books & Art, 1978. First and only edition. 217 p.
In response to a letter written to the International Netsuke
Collectors Society Journal, and based on their own frustration in
using "Legend," the authors produced an analytical subject index to
Henri Joly's essential reference work "Legend in Japanese Art." A
limited edition of 2000 copies. Hard Cover with Dust Jacket. New.
$35.00
