Anthony,

I also have purchased from Rich's sources (he is a
sukashi tsuba slut by the way). In addition I have
purchased from Ron. I would purchase from all of them
again. Many tsuba are not papered, it is not worth
the cost of many to put them through shinsa. Since it
is much harder to attribute to a particular smith, you

(for the unsigned ones). There are all price points.

you end up with all these books you can't read. Of
course, if you can read them, I will tell you which
ones to buy and you can translate them for me.

member a few years ago). Milt can give you a

billion or so people minus one).

collectors to handle tsuba. Some like kinko some like
sukashi, some like both.

that any preying mantis themed tsuba and/or sword

fun!

Re: [nihontostudy] Where do you buy your Tsuba?

by davidzincavage

Kinko refers to soft metal work tsuba. Sukashi refers to pierced tsuba.

There are certainly no rules as to what designs are best to collect. If you lack opinions, you should collect books. I recommend starting with Sasano and Torigoye's books. You might want to own the Sukashi and Akasaka tusba Sano Museum catalogues as well. And it's nice to have the Haynes catalogues. Even just those books will represent a substantial investment, so think twice, before proceeding.

The only Nihonto material certain to hold its value and be easily resellable is very expensive top quality stuff. Top quality tsuba are not necessarily easily identified or defined, but pieces of the sort sell at auction at Christie's and Sotheby's for thousands of dollars. Not uncommonly for more than $5000.

David Zincavage
