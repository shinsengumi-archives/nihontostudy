WWII vet bring back

by edwinyoung500@ymail.com

I have recently acquired a WWII Trophy, a WWII Veteran bring back Tanto.

This Japanese Tanto may be of the Osoraku Zukuri blade form. It was found
on the body of Commander Goichi Oya (Commander of Japanese forces on Tinian
Island during WW II), who committed Seppuku in a cave on the Island during
the US invasion. The body and the Tanto were found by a US Marine named
Fred Osgood who was awarded the Bronze Star and a Purple Heart. He sent the
Tanto home in 1944 after the action on Tinian Island, and it was in his
possession since his return from the war until November of 2012 when it
came into my possession.

The Tanto is 13 1/16" long with 3 3/8" being the Nakago (As measured from
the Tsuba notch). It is 0.229" thick just in front of the notch. The Nakago
tapers to 0.115 at it's end (See attached file image).

At this point I am wondering if any one has more information on this
particular, or type of, Tanto.

What I have found so far is that Commander Giochi Oya was A Commander in
the Imperial Japanese Navy, and Commander of the IJN 56th guard Unit, with
his head Quarters at Mt. Lasso, Tinian Island. He was responsible for the
defenses of the four air fields on Tinian Island. There on Tinian Island he
committed Seppuku August 2, 1944.

Thanks in advance for any information available,

Ed.
