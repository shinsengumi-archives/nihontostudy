Re: [nihontostudy] maple leaves/deer horn motif

by yamaimojg

Hi Ron,
I haven't handled that tsuba, but Kyo Shoami seems like a good call. I don't think I've seen one lacquered like that before.

Deer and maple leaves are a fairly common autumn motif. More specifically deer are associated with one of the principle deities at Kasuga jinja in Nara. There are a lot of deer at Kasuga shrine and elsewhere in Nara that are rounded up in the fall to have their antlers sawn off. So, I think that the antler with the maple branches and leaves was intended to evoke Kasuga jinja in particular, the hills around Nara and autumn in general.

I checked Google for Kasuga jinja links - this site touches on the deer:
http://fischer.jinkan.kyoto-u.ac.jp/soramitsu/DivineDeer.html

Milt, I think that it is an antler at the bottom left (looking at the front view) below the kozuka ana. The rest are maple branches.
Jim
