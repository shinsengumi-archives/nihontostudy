The styles of Aizu Shoami and Mito are very similar IMHO.

superior. Both schools did a lot of copying of other

That type of workmanship and tagane-mei are typical of the type of Nara-copy shiiremono tsuba that Bob Haynes called "Mito Shoami" in his auction catalogs. Ken,
There are signed Aizu Shoami tsuba. Some of them are fairly good quality, and others are pretty poor. It's certainly possible that the "Mito" shiiremono tsuba could be made by Aizu workers, but the workmanship is a bit different from even the low end signed Aizu work. We're probably not going to find any signed shiiremono examples to settle the issue. I think that Aizu Shoami is a better label than Mito Shoami, since we do have documented Shoami makers in Aizu (but not in Mito), but I'm not sure it's correct. Ultimately, they're copying the Nara style.

Jim
