Yoshitake wakizashi with picture of owner

by jswords03

Dear All

I bought a wakizashi this week. It is a 54.9 cm (21 5/8 inch)that is
bout of polish with chips in the ha (sure hope they are not to deep).

The blade is signed Heianjo ju Fujiwara Yoshitake.

There are a number of things that make this an interesting sword.

1. The shingunto mounts have been lacquered the dark green often seen
on the army mounts but this lacquer is painted over same and a
wood saya. The same is lifting way from the wood so you can
see the method of construction.
I knew that kai-gunto had lacquer over same but not shin-gunto.

Has anyone seen this type of construction on a shin-gunto?

2. There is a silver (silver like) metal star on the tassle. I have
never seen anything attached to tassles.

Has anyone seen a star on a tassle? What might it mean???

3. When I went to pick up the sword. The wife of the gave me a picture.
She said that her late husband did not talk much about his time
during WWII but she pointed to the person standing in the back
row to the right (looks like army uniform) and said that was
the owner of the blade.

CAn anyone translate what is written on the back of the picture?

You can see images at

http://www.montanairon.com/swords.html

then click on #7 Images to view and it will the the top sword.

Thanks for any help.

later
david (I plan to post this on a couple of email lists)
