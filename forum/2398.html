Re: [nihontostudy] Wakizaki question

by Robert Gilmore

Patrik,

You can't tell much from the photos. Unfortunately you don't include a
photo of the kisaki. The simple iron mounts could indicate a very old
piece. They could also indicate one of those very low quality arsenal
pieces they kept to use as loaners to the ashigaru. The blade looks
like it needs a full polish. Uninspiring copper habaki. The tsuka is
badly wrapped in what looks like synthetic tsuka-ito. I usually pass on
buying anything that looks like this. I especially take a pass if they
won't let you look at the nakago. Some people think you have to destroy
the handle to get it off. Have you explained to the owner that they
just slip on and off? Unless you like the way it looks and can get it
for peanuts, my recommendation is to keep looking.

Bob

Re: [nihontostudy] Wakizaki question

by sworddad

Patrik -

As Bob recommended, unless this sword some cheap--$US200?--you should save your money and spend it more wisely. This blade length is pretty typical for WWII swords. You might also check the cutting edge right at the habaki to see if this is sharp or flat; Most (but not all) WWII blades will have a dulled "flat" spot at this point. The blade looks in terrible shape and if you are unable to "see some promise" in the sword, let the next buyer take the gamble. Leave it alone and save your money for a better sword.

Ron
