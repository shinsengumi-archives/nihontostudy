Peter,
Interesting observation. I don't think I've seen nunome on Tibetan guards either, but I can't claim to have looked at many. Are they typically multi-plate construction?

It's at: http://pictures.care2.com/view/1/743171541 photo's 1 & 2.

Come to think of it I've seen Chinese guards with a square-profile cup shape. I don't think that they had a Japanese-shaped nakago ana or marks from seppa wear, though.

The rectangular pattern on the seppa dai and the style of nunome on the rim seem like typical Hizen traits. Of course they were working with foreign styles.
