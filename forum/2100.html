Chokuto

by rayasinger

Hi all, I have been visiting with my future in-laws in Korea this
past week and while there, made an interesting antique-shop find.
This sword in question appears to be an old chokuto. From what I can
see under the corrosion and layers of soil, it is a straight, single-
edged iron blade with a nakago that ends in a half-ana. There are 2
other mekugi-ana with iron mekugi and the remnant of a copper fitting
where a fuchi would be located. The sword has a plain, round iron
tsuba with no sukashi. The question I have is, what if any
preservation should be done for this sword? The condition is very
fragile and I am not even inclined to brush off the surface dirt on
this piece. Any help is greatly appreciated...
