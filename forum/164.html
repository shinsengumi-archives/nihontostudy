Hi Matt,



The question is "Why do hizento have such thin skins ?"
- Matt


They all don't.
Some do - the kobuse construction (lower quality) where better steel is
used on the outer edge, but only in a thin layer as this was cheaper.
They can certainly have a very thin outer edge.

The sanmai construction are the better swords (Tadayoshi 1, 2, 3, 4
(maybe), 8, Masahiro 1 etc. and they have thicker steel. The better
steel is used as the core.

I am of the opinion that the Tadayoshi used imported steel as it was
readily available. That maybe why the hada is so tight. Now, how
expensive this was I have no idea - but if it was expensive, then they
could have used it in a thin outer layer (kobuse) - saving costs. The
other factor to consider is that because the hada is so tight and
flawless in Hizen blades, when you polish through it into the core
steel, it then looks awful. In other Schools you probably wouldn't
notice the difference as the outer steel hada can be coarse as well.
There might only be a slight colour variation.
