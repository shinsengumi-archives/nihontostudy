by sworddad

>It's rather surprising to think that there is >such a variety of opinion on this issue!

Yes, I think you will get enough opinions to thoroughly confuse you, if asked. It is interesting to see the shinsa team handling iron tsuba with gloves--I understand this with kinko tsuba, but iron? Over the years it seems each authority had his own opinion on what was best for caring for or handling iron tsuba. In reality, I think it comes down to common senese. If you have a "treasure" iron tsuba, maybe handling it with gloves (or at least with great respect) goes along with placing it in a quality tsuba box for safe keeping or display. It becomes more a matter of respect than safe keeping. If none of these iron plates were ever handled over the centuries, they probably wouldn't have that wonderful color and "feeling" we all treasure today.

Ron
