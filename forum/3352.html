Another kodzuka for identification ...

by drjohnplissenden

Once again I am shamelessly using your combined expertise to my own
ends.

The kodzuka illustrated in my photo album JPL is ex the Ransom
collection. Within a raised rim, on a panel of fine horizontal
shakudo nanako, is a group of fruit, flowers and vegetation depicted
in high relief. The fruit and flowers are of gold, the leaves and
stems of shakudo with gold drops of water. There is a second,
smaller group nearter to the blade. The reverse is of polished
shakudo with a segment, including the butt, of gold with delicate
draw scratching. The kodzuka is uninscribed.

This appears to be Goto work of a high quality, but I am completely
inadequate for any attemt to identify the maker solely on the basis
of the image and style.

Can anybody please help. See http://tinyurl.com/77rxt

With thanks, JPL.

John,
I've been out of town. The kozuka is in Goto style, but in the photos it looks like what is usually described as Kyo-Kinko at shinsa. The metal, carving, nanako, etc., are in the style, but not up to the level of Goto work. The urasogi is unusual in copy work.

Jim
