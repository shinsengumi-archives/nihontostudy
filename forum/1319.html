Crass Commercial Message, books and sword for sale.

by matthewhoyle2000

Gang, I have decided to start the new year off by selling part of my
treasured book collection, as well as the first sword I ever bought.
Take a moment to look this post over, some of these items are pretty
hard to come by these days. Free shipping to list members within Europe,
50% off shipping to all others. Extensive descriptions and pix available
on request.

For Sale Books:

1. Japanese Swords & Sword Furniture in the Museum of Fine Arts Boston
by Morihiro Ogawa 1987. Hardback. This is a big heavy book that is quite
rare now. Over 400 items listed in 423 pages. Lots of juyo quality
stuff, making this a super reference book. Very good copy with very good
dustjacket. Some slight fading to the spine (dj) and some very small
bumps only, otherwise perfect. Comes with separate glossery in mint
condition. Asking $450

2. The Armour book in Honcho-Gunkiko by Arai Hakuseki 1964. Hardback.
Rare reference book. Very good condition, with only one very small tear
to the back of the dustjacket and priceclipped. Comes with protective
brodart cover for the dj. Asking $200

3. The Hartman Collection of Japanese metalwork by Christie's, Mason and
Woods Ltd 1976. Published as a hardback with no dustjacket this book is
in very good condition also. Only slight fading to the spine, otherwise
perfect. Comes with original errata and list of estimates (loose).
Unfortunately no list of prices realised. A definate must have for any
serious tsuba or kodogu collector, the collection of tsuba within is
simply stunning. Asking $150

4. Masterpieces of the R. B. Caldwell collection of Japanese Sword
Fittings by Patrick Syz 1994. Hardback. One very tiny crease to the top
of the dustjacket (hardly noticeable), otherwise absolutely mint. Comes
with protective brodart cover for the dj. Asking $200

5. The Arts of the Japanese Sword by B.W. Robinson. First edition, 1961.
Hardback. The book that every novice sword collector should have in his
/ her collection. Very good condition, but no dustjacket. Some small
scratches to the boards and minor bumping in the corners. Asking $125

6. Nippon-To The Japanese Sword by Inami Hakusui 1948. Hardback. An
absolute Classic, this was the first English reference book written by a
noted Japanese expert. In super condition, this book is a treasure to
behold. One small tear to the dustjacket next to the spine only. No
fading, no creases, nothing. The book looks like it was published
yesterday ! Interesting little vintage pamphlet from the Japan Sword Co
selling kitchen knives inside. Comes with protective brodart cover for
the dj. Asking $250

7. Early Japanese Sword Guards; Sukashi Tsuba, by Masayuki Sasano 1972.
Hardback. Classic reference book on sukashi tsuba this usually retails
in the $225 - 300 range depending on condition. Hoever this example is
ex libris and has the usual library stamps inside. When it was in the
library the dustjacket was stuck to the inside of the book(!). This has
since been remedied and the dustjacket is now loose and comes in a
protective brodart cover but you can still see where it used to be stuck
on. Otherwise in good - very good condition. Asking $175

8. The Carlo Monzino Collection of Japanese Sword Fittings and Swords by
Sotheby's 1996. Wonderful hardback auction catalog in mint condition.
122 pages of outstanding pieces. Asking $60

9. Japanese Master Swordsmiths: The Gassan Tradition by The Museum of
Fine Arts, Boston 1989. Softcover in very fine condition. Must have
reference for any Gassan enthousiast. Asking $75

10. Tosogu, Treasure of the Samurai by Sarzi-Amade Limited, 1991. Scarce
catalog full of tsuba and other sword fittings, very lavishly
illustrated. In extremely good condition and very hard to come by. This
is recommended for any kodogu lover. Comes with protective brodart cover
for the dj. Asking $125

11. Tsuba II: Returned Tsuba from Europe, by Inami Tomihiko. Hardback.
This is the second of a set of two describing various tsuba that were
returned from Germany and Europe to Japan. Both are very hard to find.
An interesting book with 200 tsuba illustrated in this volume, most in
b/w but also quite a few in color. Be warned this book is in Japanese
though! Extremely good condition all round. Comes with protective
brodart cover for the dj. Asking $100

12. Illustrated catalogues of Tokyo National Museum Sword guard(s) by
Tokyo National Museum, 1979. Hardback. Another scarce / rare book
illustrating 542 tsubas in the Tokyo National Museum. Black and white
pictures unfortunately, but still a must have reference for any serious
tsuba collector. I saw one copy in tatty condition sell on ebay last
year for something like $300 (!). This copy is in very good to fine
condition. Comes with protective brodart cover for the dj. Asking $175


Right and now on to the sword that I have for sale. This Nambokucho
(14th century) sword was purchased last year from Aoi Arts in Japan. It
comes with fairly recent NBTHK Tokubetsu Hozon papers, a professional
oshigata done at Aoi Arts and their own menu shaped certificate
describing the sword and stating that it was then valued at 1,2 million
yen. The sword is in a shirasaya, no koshirae and is in excellent
polish. I was toying with the idea of submitting it to Juyo Shinsa, but
have so far not got round to it. I don't know if it would stand a chance
of going Juyo, but I think that it should be submitted if only just for
the experience which would be very educational in my opinion. It is
attributed to the Nakajima Rai school which seems to be pretty darn
rare. At least this is the only one I have ever seen. Below is the info
exactly as on the Aoi Arts certificate.

'We certify the following Katana is genuine. Detail, Katana: the NBTHK
Tokubetsu-hozon token.
Signature: Unsigned, attributed to Nakajima-Rai. The blade is polished,
in a shirasaya.
Blade length: 62.7 cm or 24.69 inches.
Sori: 1.0 cm or 0.39 inch.
Width at the hamachi: 2.98cm or 1.17 inches.
Width at the yokote: 2.11 cm or 0.83 inch.
Thickness at the hamachi: 0.74 cm or 0.29 inch.
Era: Nambokucho-period. 14th century.

Shape: A shinogi-zukuri katana. Broadish blade has plenty thickness and
the point is O-kissaki.
Jitetsu: Small mokume is combined with small itame-hada. Fine jitetsu
bears delicate jinie.
Hamon: Nie-deki. Naka-suguha hamon goes with ashi, yo, kinsuji and
sunagashi.
Boshi: Niju-ba turns back roundly on the front.

A glance at this sword shows that clearly Rai School tradition.
Acceptable o-hada appears about six inches above the hamachi. Superb
jitetsu and sensitive works on the ha are the point of appreciation.
1,200,000 Yen.'

I still have the original Aoi Arts pictures that can be sent on request.
Asking $12,500.



Thanks for taking the time to read all this and let me know if I can be
of any further help or if any of you want pix or more detailed
descriptions. matthew@...
