by yamaimojg

Ron,
I've read several tellings of this anecdote, but the easiest for me to find is from Haynes and Torigoye's Tsuba an Aesthetic Study:

"The term Ko-Hagi is in reality a misnomer. Its origin will be found in a statement made by the late Morishiro concerning a rubbing of a tsuba in his collection. Akiyama noted on the oshigata that the tsuba was probably like old Choshu work (Ko-Hagi saku). From this notation it was deduced that this style of tsuba was actually made in Choshu and was the old style of work from that province. Inamura Choga took this term from Morishiro and introduced it to the public as the school of Ko-Hagi tsuba. The truth is, the tsuba in this style were made in Kyoto and are the product of the Kyo-sukashi school."

Dr. Torigoye was a student of Akiyama.
Jim
