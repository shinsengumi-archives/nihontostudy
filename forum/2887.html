maple + deer design tsuba

by bungotakadasword

Hi David,
You are interested in this particular design..........
while looking at the lundgren collection book, tsuba #126, is a ko-
kinko tsuba with maple + deer design.
Comments by Ogasawara and Kobayashi did not mention anything about
Takeda and Uesugi, but they called this theme " Kasugano ".
Just thought you may want to know.

milt

by soshuju

Milt-
You are so cute sometimes :-) In Chinese the character is
haru (Spring) but in this context is read Kasu, hi becomes ga and
hara becomes no (field). One reason, perhaps, David couldn't find any
info without the specific kanji.
As it happens I spoke to Sasama Yoshihiko Sensei the other
day and he also had not heard of any such connections between this
design and the aforementioned persons. However another friend said
that the association is connected to another design with similar
elements. I haven't an example I can point to but I was told that
when you see a single kaede leaf in opposition to a single horn
presented equal in size, the theme is still Kasuga. However there is
the added association with Takeda Shingen and Uesugi Kenshin because
the two elements in the design like the two famous generals were
equal neither able to defeat the other. He couldn't cite any sources
and it was just something he had heard. I hope to ask other
knowledgeable people in the next couple of days just to see, if they
too have heard something similar...
-t
