Rich -

Interesting to see this tsuba. My Akasaka of this design signed
Tadashige (to my relief) passed shinsa in Chicago. Of course I have it
here in front of me now to compare the work with your tsuba. While the
design is the same, the work on mine is more delicate (?)--more
piercing--and seems to "flow" more, if you know what I mean. The
horse's hoofs on your tsuba are more "squared" and "bold" than the more
naturally shaped hoofs on my tsuba. I just saw a similar tsuba
(picture) just the other day that showed these rather boldly made hoofs
and it was called something other than Akasaka, but for the life of me
I don't recall where I saw this. Thought it was in the new Mosle'
catalogue, but if so, I'm not finding it now. My tsuba came out of a
large collection of tsuba sold in Santiago, Chile in 2003. That
collection also had another similar "horse" tsuba that had these large
hoofs like your new tsuba, but I didn't buy that one. Most of these
730-plus tsuba were bought up by Michael Zomber, so perhaps your's
could be the one I saw. I'll check my records.

Ron
