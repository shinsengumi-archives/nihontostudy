This scan is from the book 'Tsuba in Southern California', pg. 32
bottom right. This tsuba apparently belonged to Robert Haynes and I
believe it had the ume added when the box/hako gaki was done
(conjecture).
I did some research into this tsuba mei and I believe it is Myochin
Ki Muneharu, Haynes 06041.0 in that the kanji for Haru matches that
of the mei most closely. Unfortunately I do not have Katchushi
Meikan by Sasama,Y. so I cannot verify this attribution. In
researching the mei I found these to be the closest:
Muneyoshi: Haynes 06365.0 Kinko Meikan new: pg. 436
old: pg. 535 - 536
Wakayama TKMT-III: pg. 270 - 271
Muneharu: Haynes 06049.0 Kinko Meikan new: pg. 427
old: pg. 525
Of note is the teganemei, although subtle, point to Muneyoshi.
However, the mei simply does not match any of the examples found in
any of the above texts.
It would be of interest to see the mei from the Sasama book which
perhaps another member might have on hand.
