umegane-good or bad?

by Peter A Bleed

Listeros!
Allow me to once again present the limits of knowledge with a
question that others may find either 1) of interest or 2) answerable.
Yesterday I looked at a "sword" brought home from the Philippines and
owned (proudly) by the nephew of the man who sent it home in 1944. Since
this could be anything and not likely for sale, I think we will all agree
that the chances of this being of any interest were pretty slim. Surprise,
it turned out to b a relatively clean wakizashi in a nice old shira-saya.
The blade was signed Fujishima Tomoshige, I assume one of the late
koto/very early Shinto Kaga smiths of that line - tight itame with a
komidare. A sword.
Perhaps the most interesting thing about the sword was a rectangular
umegane set into the shinogi-ji in the monouchi. It measured maybe 1/4" by
1/2" and was clearly visible. It had not fully removed the flaw since there
was a clear fukure on its edge.
My question to the List is whether on not this kind of umegane is
good or bad. Well, it can hardly be "good," right? There was a flaw there,
but an umegane must be better than a flaw, right? Does an umegane detract
from or enhance the value of a sword? And how visible should an umegane
be? Is the fact that this plug was clearly visible - and square! - mean
that it was poorly done?
Peter Bleed

RE: [nihontostudy] umegane-good or bad?

by robsongord

Hi Peter,

I don't know if this has been answered yet, but, in general umegane are
always bad, particularly if they are easy to spot. In a blade like this
Fujishima where open, flowing grain in the shinogi-ji is to be expected,
doing an umegane is just silly. The same is true with a very fine omi-yari I
own that is hugh. It also has a series of rectangular umegane that have
mostly fallen out. The blade is Kanabo and one should expect powerful
running masame that opens up in places in such a work. There is no reason to
do umegane and now that they are visible and have fallen out, it detracts a
great deal from the blade. On the other had, I have a rather nice katana
where the umegane had fallen out. I had it replaced. You can't see it and it
looks better now as opposed to the open hole in the skin. So, a case by case
answer. Just my 25 cents worth.

Gordon
