Mark,

I have both books. Yumoto's is a magnificent pocket reference,
although a bit dated. The Connoisseur's is a much more detailed
reference, covering the same ground as Yumoto's, but with more
examples of every feature a nihonto can have. Curiously enough, every
now and then I see it being offered on eBay, sometimes reaching bid
amounts above 100 dollars. For the life of me, I can't understand
why. Not that the book isn't worth it, but because it's still
available off the shelf at Amazon for less than 50 bucks:
http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/redirect?path=ASIN/4770020716&link_code=as2&camp=1789&tag=lairofthenecr-20&creative=9325

Now, that being said, the Connoisseur's is probably the best
reference available in English. It pales in comparison to published
in Japan nihonto reference books which can make the Connoisseur's
look like the pocket reference version.

Lastly, another book I found to be a good read, especially as it
covers nihonto manufacture, is The Craft of the Japanese Sword, by
Leon Kapp, also available from Amazon:
http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/redirect?path=ASIN/087011798X&link_code=as2&camp=1789&tag=lairofthenecr-20&creative=9325

Hope that helps.

Regards,

Andres

Mark,
I second Andres suggestion of the Connoisseur's book, no doubt the best in
English. Here are a couple other cheap books in English worth having: The
Japanese Sword: A Comprehensive Guide by Kanzan Sato, usually found cheap at
Amazon or other book sellers;
JAPANESE SWORDSMITHS: Commemorative Centenary Edition by W.Hawley can be
found at www.satcho.com or other book sellers, not perfect, but the best
available in English.
Modern Japanese Swordsmiths; 1868-1945, by John Slough available for about
$100 is well worth it, if you are into WWII gendaito, showato blades. Hope
that helps,
Mike
