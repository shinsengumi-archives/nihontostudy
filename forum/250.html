Re: [nihontostudy] tsuba question

by yamaimojg

Milt,
They're grouped under Heianjo, but they are a consistent sub-style. They have those deeply hammered plates almost like Tembo work, and the brass inlay motifs vary but don't seem to directly relate to the forming of the plate. The shape of the kogai ana is also very consistent within this type. I'm not aware of any signed examples that would give a clue as to how they might relate to the other types of brass inlay guards lumped together under Heianjo zogan. They seem to date to Momoyama to early Edo. Yours is on the later end of the range.

I've never seen a wisteria with leaves like that.

Milt,
Wisteria have compound leaves that look somewhat like sumac or ash. A google image search on wisteria will turn up lots of examples. Your two guards are probably kiri motif. The one in the eBay link is the same type, but probably younger yet, although it looks like it may have been cleaned a bit enthusiastically.

I hadn't thought of it before, but the sukashi openings in this one combined with the hammering of the plate reminds me of Shoami Shigenobu's plate work. I don't know if there is any relationship there. I can't think of any heianjo-type brass inlay work by Shigenobu.
