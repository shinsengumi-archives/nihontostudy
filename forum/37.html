stolen sword alert

by samurai@xhasc.com

A Tokubetsu Juyo Yukimitsu was stolen in transit from me to Japan.

Last year a Juyo Token Niji Kunitoshi was taken in the same manner, it
was scanned by someone as being sent to Canada Customs, then it
vanished. There was a several week period in both cases of me having to
fight people who's computer screens told them something that wasn't
true, then finally they admitted the package vanished into thin air.

Last year they searched the warehouse several times without it turning
up, and when I said I was sending the police over, 15 minutes later the
sword appeared in some office where someone had "mistakenly left it"
after scanning that it was sent to customs.

My hope is that the same scam is being played again.

Obviously with the cost of a blade like this, it is not a trivial matter
and I feel kind of sick. Insurance says they will not cover it.

Pictures of the blade are on my site as I have the paperwork.

It has gone missing or been stolen in the Toronto area. Lately there
has been a new study group formed in Toronto with a lot of attendees. It
is possible with the new dissemination of information there that there
is more knowledge about the value of these swords and so we're going to
start seeing more thefts.

Here's the link to my site:

http://www.nihonto.ca/yukimitsu-2/

Please, everyone be on the lookout for someone who is asking questions
about an antique sword that they have found, and are trying to get the
value of that matches the pictures and description on my site. If
someone presents it to you in person, please take it and call the police
immediately.

- darcy

group or it's members in this thread. It seems a perfectly logical
conclusion that as knowledge of the intrinsic value of items is spread,
more interest will be directed towards those items from people of shady
character. This does not reflect upon the group disseminating the
