Kanenobu and deleted kanji

by jswords03

Dear All

I have a special order Gendaito by Kanenobu. This is the Gendaito smith
that became a intangible cultural asset of Gifu prefecture in 1973. It
turns out that part of the kanji, on the right hand side of the ura side
of the nakago, have been deleted.

The left hand side I think translates as

"This blade was presented to the person, who's name has been deleted,
for his sincere assistance in the shipping department at the the
Hankengoku Educational Institution located by the Kizugawa (Kizu River)
in Kyoto, in Greater Japan. "

This is with help from Harry Watson (thanks) but any mistakes are all my
doing.

On the right hand side of the ura all that is left is something like

"This blade was presented to someone of rank that could be called His
Majesty"

<>Many of the kanji have been erased/deleted.

You can see images of the nakago and the kanji that I have identified at

http://www.montanairon.com/images2.html

<>Now my questions are

1. Does any one know anything about this Hankengoku Educational Institution?

2. Is there enough information still on the nakago to identify who the
blade was presented to?

3. Is there a way to raise the deleted kanji?

Like they do one guns with the serial numbers filed off!

<>
Thanks for any help.



later

david
