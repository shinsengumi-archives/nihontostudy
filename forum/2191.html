hi Steve,
Personally, I only clean the opening IF the rust distort the outline
of the design but don't overdo it..........if in doubt best to leave
it alone.
Another issue is " waxing " the tsuba.
I've heard many conflicting views on that............I suspect a lot
of people do that to give their tsuba that lustrous moist look, but
of course few will admit to that.
I have outbid a well known /knowledgable dealer on a can of old
japanese tsuba wax on e-bay........question is if the advance people
poo poo on waxing the tsuba then why he placed a bid ?

Naturally you guys will ask if I wax my tsuba.........hahahaa, yes
and no. I used to but I found out the wax does wear off so the
effect of the lustre is not permanent. I discovered a better way now,
no, it's not wearing it in your rear pocket ( for the SF residents ).

milt

A friend of mine who worked in a gym (sweated a lot) used to occasionally take one of my iron tsuba and wear it around his neck, day and night. Yes, he even slept with it! (Lord only knows...) Two of these tsuba took on a wonderful color within a month while another--it seems it was a later tsuba of a more dense/harder plate--did not color much. Like Cary's tsuba from years ago--tsuba in the pocket treatment--sometimes natural oils (juices) work, sometimes they don't.

by yamaimojg

BTW,
If anyone does feel the need to wax an iron tsuba, be careful to keep the wax out of the sukashi openings. It'll get into any rust there and will not look right.

Overall, wax is better than oiling a guard (which really makes a mess), but it shouldn't be necessary for a well treated tsuba, at least in a moderate climate.

Jim
