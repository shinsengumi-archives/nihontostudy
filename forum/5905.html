Well, I once went to see a collection of Japanese swords at the request of a man who got my name and phone number from the Japanese Consulate. He was a very pleasant man who had been collecting 3 years and had 25 swords. They were all Chinese repros. Every last Mother of all Fakes one of them. Not a real Japanese blade there. Having said that, there were a very few that were worthy of the generic name "sword" as they actually looked good. They had hamon and hada and a passable sugata. But they were not Nihonto.

Jim, I'll still bet on the basis of your one photo that your cheap estate pickup is a recent Chinese crapper. It is a hard call without the blade in hand, but here's looking forward to being found wrong...

Hello:

The more I look at it the more it looks like a non-Japanese origin sword
that was passed off as what it isn't..the lack of a Yokote, along with a
couple of other things bug me..I paid a whopping $20.00 for this and well, it's
worth that to me as a "What NOT to buy" example for my Iai and Batto
students, still it has a decent Hamon on it... I had a good idea it wasn't an
original..I basically took it cause it does have a nice Hamon.

Now the ones that I am finishing up making for book IV will in no way ever
be confused with ANYTHING original simply by the way I am signing them and
the method of polish...You can get a really NICE polish ("working" type)
just by using wet/dry paper up to 2500 grit wet and then hit it with 5000
pumice in an oil suspension. NOT for real swords but for "modern Japan-ish"
stuff that I am making for cutting up mats and the like...shows nice
structures within the blade as well as the Hada on the laminated ones..

I will just stash this one away for the above purposes....

Thanks all
