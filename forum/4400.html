The Conscience of the Collector

by elliottshogun

I just read for the 1st time, 'The Conscience of the Collector' by
Robert E. Haynes. "Right On"!

Robert states "All of the large group meetings held in the West, for
the collectors of swords and fittings, are not of an intellectual
nature but almost purely of a material dealer/merchant nature; this has
been so for over forty years." Does that hit home or what? I will have
my second show of attendance this coming weekend.

I hope to attend all of the lectures, it's the only way to continue my
education. I have to admit, I will have a table this year. I will have
Tsuba (I went overboard collecting tsuba), sword bags and stands (for
tsuba and swords) for sale. All of my items for sale are 'Hand Made'.
But my intention is not to attend shows to sell. I want to attend shows
to LEARN.

So, I hope to meet Robert E. Haynes and tell him "I agree, there are
too many in this field who are only business oriented."
