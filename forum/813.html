Tosa Myochin tsuba - cont.

by retalt

Hi all, still chasing up info on these Tosa tsuba smiths. I am getting more information slowly but am still finding differences here and there.

I put up an image of Cary's Munitoshi tsuba that is signed TOSA no KUNI ju KII MYOCHIN MUNETOSHI. It is an excellent piece
and the mei confirms that it is a Tosa Myochin guard by Munetoshi. Cary found the listing for this maker in Haynes, (Index of Sword Fittings & Associated Artists). (H 06291.0) Haynes notes that he died ca. 1700. He also notes that he was a student of Akasaka Tadatoki
(H 09165.0) When cross referencing to this Akasaka Tadatoki, Haynes states that he was the first Akasaka Tadatoki and was considered
the 4th generation of the main Akasaka line. Haynes notes that he died in 1746.

This would seem to be in conflict with Sasano's research. In his Early Japanese Sword Guards (Sukashi Tsuba) book (1972) on page 22, he discusses the Tosa Myochin line. He dates them to late Edo and mentions that they started as students of the 7th generation Akasaka Tadatoki.

Can someone reconcile the dating of Tosa Myochin guards in light of the conflict between Sasano and Haynes.

Cheers all
