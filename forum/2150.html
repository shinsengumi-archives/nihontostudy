by tantojim

A visitor to my site was asking about a listing of the meanings of the Nengo
eras. I have many books that list the eras and time period covered, but
even the dictionaries I have found don't give the meaning for "Kanbun",
"Eisho", etc. Does anyone know of a good source for this? I could make
educated guesses at many of them but assume there is a compiled list out
there somewhere. I have looked in Papinot and all of my usual sources.
Any help would be appreciated.

Re: Nengo Meanings

by incu903

NENGOs are made by picking out (usually) 2 kanji characters
from sentences in ancient Chinese books.

For example, current nengo is HEISEI(HEI + SEI).

If your computer can read Japanese 2-byte characters.
please see the explanation below.

「平成」の名前の由来は、史記五帝本紀の「内平かに外成る（内平外成）」、
書経大禹謨の「地平かに天成る（地平天成）」からで「内外、天地とも平和が
達成される」という意味。


HEISEI was picked up from 2 different sentences in two different
ancient Chinese books. These two sentences consist of HEI, SEI and
other characters.

And the meanings of the original sentences are:
1. Peacefulness will be attained both in inner world and outer world.
2. Peacefulness will be attained both on earth and in heaven.

Without this knowledge, no one can tell the meaning of "HEISEI".
Because there is no sush word as HEISEI in Japanese language.
