by dhs1us

There are two stories where frogs (or toads) crop up, that I know of, in Japanese imagery. One is
with Gama Senin- a mythical sage who is often seen with a toad on his shoulder. Sort of his
familiar.
The other is the story of Ono-no-tofu, whereby a would-be civil servant is encouraged to try once
again at taking his exams after watching a frog perservere in his attempts to jump out of a pond,
up a steep bank. A frog could therefore be seen as a symbol of perserverance.


-Doug Sanders
