Nobuiye

by sworddad

Hi All -

Thought I'd through this out for discussion...

The tortuous shell pattern (also referred to as simply "hexigan" motif) is found on many of the Nobuiye revival made tsuba -- often, tosho tsuba -- of shinshin-to times. Looking at examples of Nobuiye work, this motif does frequently appear on his tsuba. Has anyone ever found reference to WHY this motif was so often used by Nobuiye? Does it relate to something that was particularly meaningful to Nobuiye? Does anyone ever recall reading anything on this? It would seem doubtful that it was used simply because it was a pleasing pattern to adorn tsuba.
