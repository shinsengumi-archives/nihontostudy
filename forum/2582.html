What to think on these tsuba?

by kdw21620

Hi all,

My second mantis tsuba ever was stripped off a shinto
wakasashi that seemed to be in period mountings. The
blade was buffed and was eventually resold minus the
tsuba. I just got this 2nd tsuba the other day. I am
attaching the photo, however, if Jim didn't allow
attachements I will post it. I was given attributions
of either ko shoami or akasaka mid edo. I know these
aren't of the highest quality, but I am seeking any
comments that can be made about them.

I don't neccesarily see the signs of casting that
normally are seen, ie bubbles around the seppa dai,
etc. However, barring the broken antanae (the 1st one
owned) and the wheel leg they match exactly. The back
pattern of the mantis appears to have some different
low relief detail carving crosshatch vs lines. I
think the one on the left (newest acquisition) might
need a really good cleaning which obscures some of it
plus it has some rust pitting here or there. Any
comments on them and or manufacture of them?

hopefully these pics won't take too long to load, I
just got high speed wireless internet access and I am
enjoying extremely fast download seconds!
Thanks,
Ken aka "the mantis dude"
