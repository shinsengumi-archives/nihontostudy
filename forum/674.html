Re: [nihontostudy] Re: tsuba school

by dhs1us

I am new to the group and am enjoying reading about the nuances of tsuba connoiseurship and
collecting. As an art conservator (though generally art on paper, not metals) I'm able to give a
little bit of professional info on this one.
In the museum world, the general method for labeling objects is to first put down a coat, acting
as a barrier layer, of some sort of clear polymer. Nowadays it is usually a non-yellowing product
called paraloid B-72 which is soluble in toluene and/or xylene. Sometimes, clear nail polish is
used which is soluble in acetone. The ID# is then painted on this, and a second coat of the
polymer is used to make everything stay put.
I suppose some private collectors wouldn't hesitate to just use something like white-out or
liquid-paper for numbering. This will chip off easily. In instances where things are more
difficult, and you risk scratching the delicate metal surface, try a q-tip swab with a little of
the above mentioned solvents and it should do the trick.

Regards,
Doug Sanders
