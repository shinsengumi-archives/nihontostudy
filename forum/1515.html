Re: [nihontostudy] shinsa question

by yamaimojg

Cary,
Good to hear your voice on the list again.

Based on my limited experience with overseas NTHK shinsa, the criteria are similar. One difference is that submissions for koshirae shinsa, including tsuka-only submissions, will have the signatures of the kodogu elements noted where visible, but will not get attributions of maker or time period for any unsigned kodogu elements.

So, Milt's tsuka, if genuine, would be described in detail as to materials, carving and inlay techniques, decorative motifs, and be measured and photographed, but would not receive maker attributions for the menuki or fuchigashira. A date and point value for the koshirae as a unit will be provided, but not for the individual kodogu elements.

Agreed that a tsuba and tsuka without a saya is not an appropriate submission for koshirae shinsa.

A customer can submit a complete koshirae for shinsa and then, for example, submit the mumei tsuba separately as kodogu if an attribution of maker, time period and point value is desired. This requires two submissions, though.

An advantage of overseas shinsa is that we can immediately contact the owner of the item if there is a problem with the submission. For example, if a tsuka is submitted for koshirae shinsa and the fuchigashira are genuine but the menuki are reproductions, we will track down the owner and advise him that the item will fail as koshirae due to the menuki, but that if he would like to change the submission (no additional charge) to kodogu shinsa of the fuchigashira, it can be certified. We "saved" a nice Omori Teruhide F/K this way.

Jim

Hi Cary and Milt,



This is good timing as I wanted to warn Chicago shinsa attendees about
submitting koshirae. If you submit a tsuka, then the same will apply as with
the NBTHK. Make sure that the tsuka has not been re-wrapped in the last 100
or so years - that means it is no longer antique and will be bounced. Entire
koshirae will be judged based on authenticity and age of all the pieces,
i.e. nothing replaced, re-wrapped or re-lacquered. Also, if pieces are
mumei, they will not be attributed. You must submit the pieces separately
for that. If all or some of the pieces are signed, then they will be
authenticated.
