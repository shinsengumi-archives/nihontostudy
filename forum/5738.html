Papers

by maiorian

On the subject of papers collectors should be aware that one or two
unscrupulus Japanese dealers have been entering blades with Tokubetsu
Kicho papers into western auction houses, these blades having been refused
by recent (Hozon etc) shinsa panels, some of these blades were by big
names. I stopped this practice a few years ago at Christie's London but it
still takes place on occasion elsewhere.

Alan

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Papers

by retalt

Hi all, I think what Alan is alluding to is that there is fair
evidence that at some time in I think the 70's or 80's, at one of the
outer Tokyo branches, there was quite a bit of influence being exerted
by some unsavoury criminal elements on some of the judges, to get their
swords through, and this resulted in green papers being issued on swords
they shouldn't. That at least is how I understand it.

It was also only after a certain time, like in the last 10 years that
they were in existence, or something like that.

I have always been of the opinion that if it has a green paper, re
submit it for a Hozon. Don't forget that new evidence on a smith or
school could have since come to light and or new thinking may be in
place and you may get a different result regardless if the paper is good
or bad.

Cheers

Rich
