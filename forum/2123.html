Re: [nihontostudy] Re: looking for a book

by dougblain

May I ask why you want this book? I had a copy for a number of years, but finally sold it. I found the diagrams to be fairly poor and there is a very limited number of smiths listed in the book, so completeness is an issue. Also the descriptions of the works were sparce. In general, I didn't find the book very useful and I seem to remember that the book isn't cheap. A better investment, in my opinion, is some of the larger Zaikans such as the one by Imura, which list a large number of smiths sorted by eras (Koto, Shinto, Shinshinto) and the oshigatas clearly show the hamon, boshi, nakago and signature (and hada in some cases, but it's hard to do). These can be costly, but they really allow you to verify whether the blade you have is by that smith.
