Nice tsuba, Dirk. I would not call this Kaga work, more likely Yoshiro or simply Heianjo zogan (if not Yoshiro). The kogai hitsuana looks very Kyoto to me. I also think Yoshiro work was "outlined" with chisel marks...not sure about that. (Maybe that was a way to distinguish between Yoshiro and Onin? Suspect Onin work was done that way.) Sorry to be sound so confusing here. Nice overlapping zogan...my guess is Yoshiro.

Hi Alan -

Sorry. When I said Kaga and the Yoshiro, I meant Kaga Yoshiro and the Yoshiro of Kyoto area. (I think that's right, right?)

When thinking Kaga Yoshiro, I've had the impression that more often they would have squarish-type hitsuana; these are conventional shape with the kogai histsu being very "rounded/drilled-like" as you see on many of the Kyo-sukashi tsuba. That was my thinking here. Not sure why, but I also thought Kaga Yoshiro tsuba were more of a thinner plate than the others, but I'm not sure where that idea came from.

Re: [nihontostudy] yoshiro school tsuba?

by Barry Thomas

Well, the karakusa leaf does remind me of Kaga-Fushimi work. My sample of ONE
was a 2 inch kojiri on a katana I once had. I saw similar work in one of Bob
Haynes' catalogues that he called Kaga Fushimi. That's the beginning and end of
my knowledge on this, except that I do have a Heianjo/Yoshiro zogan tsuba the
same as the one in Gary D. Murtha's Vol 1 (? - maybe Vol 2). Oh, in Kaga
Fushimi work the leaves were modelled in a copper-like alloy and the veins
incised, so the resemblance in this case is only superficial. I'm not making an
authoritative statement here...
Barry Thomas.
