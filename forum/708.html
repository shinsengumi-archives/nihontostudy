RE: [nihontostudy] Sadanobu or Teimei

by robsongord

Hi Rich,

Not sure what the motif is, but as for the way names are read, typically the
kunyomi is used or the Japanese pronunciation. However, even sword-smiths
are given onyomi or Sino-Japanese nicknames. Perhaps the most famous is
Nagayoshi, who is typically called Chogi. There is also Hiroyoshi, whose
nickname is Hiroga. Teimei may be a Japanese nickname for Sadanobu. I am
pretty sure you would read his signature using the kunyomi. On the other
hand, there are lots of fittings makers whose name is read using the onyomi
-- Tomei, Somin, all the Goto guys. Even the Omori guys' names are
pronounced here in Japan using the onyomi. I hope this is sufficiently
confusing.

Gordon

Re: Sadanobu or Teimei

by klepet33319

Kishu no Ju Sadanaga aka Teimei. I believe the theme may be a foot
bridge although traditionally they have five planks if memory serves.

see Haynes 07752.0 pp 1559-1560 -- A group of artists who all signed,
Kishu ju Sadanaga. There were at least four or five generations.
They seem to have come from the early Edo Hoan school. Some read
this name as Teimei. Furukawa catalog pl. 27 #153.
SCE. W - 496-L-2, W - III - 159 - 161.
