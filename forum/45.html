Re: katana vs wakizashi

by alchemyst

Ken -

Presence of kogai and kozuka ana are maybe part of the
story. I asked Jim a similar question and as I understood
his reply (perhaps he will elaborate); katana tsuba
normally have both kozuka and kogai ana or either
while wakizashi tsuba normally only have a kozuka ana.
Although there are numerous exceptions; small sukashi
tsuba which to my eye are obviously for a wakizashi
based on size not uncommonly have both. Also many
of the very early tosho and katchushi tsuba had
only one (many had been added later). I hope I haven't
done too much damage to what Jim had said. I'm sure
he will fill in the blanks.

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: katana vs wakizashi

by yamaimojg

Hi,
Just time for a partial answer. From early koshirae I have seen, katana are mounted with kogai only, kozuka and kogai, or neither. I have not seen a katana mounted with only a kozuka.

Wakizashi are very commonly mounted with only a kozuka, but are also seen with kozuka and kogai.

The two files in the nihontostudy website File section include columns to indicate the kozuka and kogai configuration for each mounting in the Katana Koshirae catalog.

We see a fair number of 8+ cm tsuba around with a kozuka ana cut in them... were they really big wakizashi tsuba, were katana mounted with kozuka only at some point or was the ana used for a kogai, despite the kozuka shape? I don't know. There is one wakizashi koshirae in the spread sheet with an 8.6 cm tsuba, so that might be the explanation.

I'll come back to this later.

Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: factory plate

by yamaimojg

Ken,
I don't think that the article on iron plate is on my site, just on Rich's. It might be buried in my Q&A page text.

I have seen a couple of copper or brass plates that appeared to be folded, but I think that's pretty unusual (and I'm not even sure about those). I think that the earlier ones were just hammered out into a plate without folding. Both earlier and later pieces were cast to shape. Metallurgists can identify casting vs. hammering vs. a combination of the two in copper alloy artifacts by microscopic examination of cross sections. I'm not aware of any reliable way to tell from just looking at the finished piece.

Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: katana vs wakizashi

by yamaimojg

Rich,
FWIW, the data in my spreadsheet and the dat file that Rich made from it comes from ca. late Muromachi to early Edo period mounts.

I think that katana and wakizashi with both kozuka and kogai is the more formal arrangement.

I see the katana with neither (kozuka or kogai) as the likely battle setup, or with kogai only, depending on rank. Kozuka only for wakizashi. No proof for that, though.

Looks like a kozuka-ana to me. The shape of the ana would be more indicative
of a kogai rather than the size. Some early stuff with what can be taken as
ubu-ana can be either very large or very small. Ko-Mino kogai come in a wild
variety of sizes including pencil thin ones.
