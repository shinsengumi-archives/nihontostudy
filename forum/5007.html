Hazama Tsuba & Kunitomo

by neobliviscar

Lists,

I've been studying 2 Kunitomo & 2 Hazama tsuba
recently. Having read the Koza entry, Haynes Index,
Torigoye, and a few other references- there seems to
be a lot of confusion.

Haynes Index indicates the relationship between the
Kunitomo tsuba vs those signed Hazama is unclear. The
Koza has the founder being Kunitomo Mura (no
clarification or mention of whether he ever signed)
and then Kunitomo Teihei (aka Sadashige) moving to
Kameyama and working.

Some texts state the Hazama came first, then the
Kunitomo. Other seem to have it the other way around.
Anyone able to share the current thought on these
groups. Are they one and the same, or two doing
similar work?
What is the standing and relation of Teiei
(Sadashige) to those signed Hazama? Should he be
considered the founder of a Kameyama subgroup of the
Kunitomo? His work seems the most primitive of the 4
tsuba, but then he and the other Kunitomo tsuba are
working in iron while the Hazama are in much softer
copper.

Any help much appreciated!

Curran
