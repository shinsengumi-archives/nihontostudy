The name of the motif I was thinking of is unban. "Unban is a Buddhist ritual gong. The Unban is a copper gong and beat signaled the mealtime in the temple." It was introduced from China.

Here are some pictures of these copper gongs:
http://tinyurl.com/v8lh

The motif is also found on textiles and other objects. As a decorative motif it often is sometimes depicted upside down from the functional orientation. The suspension hole is sometimes drawn as an inome also.

Here are the sites the images came from. You'll need to scroll down a bit.
