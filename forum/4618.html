Interesting sword

by dwmac2000

Hello, Recently purchased a sword which was in a closet wrapped in a
blanket for the last 60+ years. It has a Shin gunto tsuka and a older
appearing Kyo-sukashi tsuba. The blade is a bit "rough" but still has
very visible hada. The hamon appears to be Suguha for the most part.
The hada is very prominant, it seems to be a Itame/Mokume mix with
Masame near the ha. The Masame hada also can be clearly seen through
the kissaki. The sword is unsigned with a osujikai yasurime. The
nakago condition appears ( a guess ) approx. 1800-1860. I'm thinking
a late Edo copy of an Koto Yamato. Nagasa 65cm, machi width 3.1 cm,
yokote 2.1 cm, sori 1.5 cm. Do these characteristics suggest to
anyone a particular Shinshinto School, Smith or Province. I know it
would be difficult with or without blade in hand, but any advice or
information would be appreciated. Thanks.....

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Interesting sword

by sworddad

Ricky -

>The hada is very prominant, it seems to be a Itame/Mokume mix with Masame near the ha. The Masame hada also can be >clearly seen through the kissaki...O-sugikai yasurime...suguha...

All of this makes me think ko-to and not at all shinshin-to work. I wonder if your sword's nakago is simply well preserved, making you think it is "newer" than it really is. Anytime I see a masame running along the ha it makes me think "early" and starts me looking for further clues. The sword is relatively short; is the nakago original (ubu) or could it be O-suriage? The mention of O-sugikai yasurime makes me think it is ubu, unsigned. It still makes me think ko-to with Yamato characteristic; sue-Tegai perhaps? You do not mention sori; I'm curious what this is telling you. Any photos available???

Ron
