Re: [nihontostudy] Sukashi tsuba

by yamaimojg

John,
As others have said, today it would probably be attributed to Katchushi. When I first glanced at the shumei I picked up on the second character as the "a" 阿 in Shoami and then on the next character as the middle kanji 乙 from Saotome. Of course the rest does not fit either case, although Saotome and Ko-Shoami would be somewhat defensible attributions. The first kanji looks like ichi/kazu 一 and the last ko 小. I can't make sense of it, but feel like I must be missing something obvious. The other character at the left appears to be a kao, although I do not know who it belongs to.

I also spent time on your gold seal when you posted it, but struck out on that one too. I don't have a tensho dictionary so I asked my shodo teacher to look it up, but couldn't conclude on any known tsubako.

They're both bugging me, so if I turn anything up I will post it. Meanwhile, hopefully someone else will take a look and settle it.

Sorry,
Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Sukashi tsuba

by yamaimojg

Dear John,
Ichi - yes, but the "mei" in H1715, I have to say no. The nichi part is not written that way in gyosho or sosho, but the three-stroke radical I mentioned before is. The getsu part is also not normally written that way even in sosho, but it's at least possible as a variant. Of course the next two ji are still there to be explained.

Haynes' reference would be to an engraved signature rather than a shumei.

In appraisal documents there is often an "ichi" as in "this item," but I haven't seen it in this sort of shumei. Of course, most if not all of the shumei I've seen on tsuba have been apocryphal, but usually to some obvious "big name."

Sorry I'm not doing better,
Jim
