Re: Kanabo color

by davidzincavage

The description on that Kanabo mentions "a strong gold sheen to the jitetsu." I happen to have a Kanabo sword myself, and I've noted the gold color of the steel myself. There is a well known generalization that says that Koto swords made with native iron have a blue to black color, and that Shinto swords made with European imported iron are gold. So why would Kanobo swords made only into the very early Shinto so commonly feature a gold-colored jitetsu? Did Gordon Robson's earlier articles in the NTHK journal (which I have not seen) address this question?
