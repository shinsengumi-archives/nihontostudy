Nihonto and Tosogu meet Star Trek...

by klepet33319

OK -- now this is a little exercise in how dissimilar interests come
together in mysterious ways. While reading up on Yagyu tsuba in
the 'Selected Fine Japanese Swords from European NBTHK Collections' I
ran across an interesting reference to Yagyu Yoshikane Renya (1625-
94) who is the founder of the Yagyu style of sword guards.
Apparently after retiring from being the fourth master of the school
he set about the design of tsuba, having them crafted by many leading
Owari tsubako of the day. His workshop was located in the village of
Kobayashi, south of Nagoya Castle, and he took the priest name of
Kobayashi Osho. Now anyone who is into Star Trek knows of the famous
Kobayashi Maru Scenerio, which is a test of an officer candidates
character in the face of certain defeat/death. 'No killing, no gain,
no win, no lose -- the sword of Shinkage Ryu is not the sword style
of outside but the style of inside -- no formal style is our style';
Yagyu Jubei Mitsuyoshi from his "Tsuki no sho".
'Admiral Kirk, who had been monitoring the situation from the control
room decides to stop the simulation. The main viewscreen slides aside
as he walks into the room. Saavik asks for suggestions, to which Kirk
replies that she should pray as the Klingons don't take prisoners.
All the "deceased" officers begin to rise, the blowers begin blowing
the smoke in the simulator room clear, and the trainees are ordered
to report for debriefing. Saavik remains and protests that the test
was unfair as there was no way to win. Kirk tells her that how a
person faced death was as important as how they faced life'; from:

Star Trek II -- The Wrath of Khan
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kobayashi_Maru

LOL -- I wonder if Gene Rodenberry was into Nihonto?
