Studying 'newbee'

by elliottshogun

I was complaining about learning nothing from shinsa (NY 2006)other
than a falling off temperline in the tip is (maybe) a fatal flaw.
Since I am definitly a 'newbee' and I study, study, study, I found
something called YAKIOTOSHI. I also studyied the JUYO BIJUTSU HIN and
TOKUBETSU JUYO blades that have this wonderful feature. Yakiotoshi is
of no concern, infact an attribute to old, Koto swords.

I am happier to find this out myself from studying than to have
nothing told to me after a shinsa. What they could have said, "This is
a Koto blade possibly made by Sukesada but we won't paper it due to
the temperline running off in the tip". Even without paper, thats
money well spent.

Elliott

Re: [nihontostudy] Studying 'newbee'

by retalt

Hi Elliot, no offence, but do not try to confuse your sword with a Juyo
or higher sword unless yours is of the same level. The hamon running off
the kissaki IS a fatal flaw, make no bones about that. Swords with fatal
flaws that make Tokubetsu Hozon, Juyo or Yashu saku are swords of
extraordinary worth, and a flaw that is fatal can not take away it's
history. These swords are typically famous because they are rare, or
were carried by someone important or famous. General swords, made by the
not so famous sword smiths, no matter how nice will most likely fail all
attempts at kanteisho if theirs is a fatal flaw like the hamon running
off the Ha or Kissaki, a Hagiri and the likes.

We all learn hard lessons and we never like being told that our hard
earned and well spent money if for naught, but this is a mugs game, it
takes nerves of steel and a firm resolve. All I can suggest is to get
over it and move on. I mean that in the nicest possible way. I am not
trying to be smart, just realistic.

Good luck

Richard Turner

Thank you Richard,

I am not upset or mad at anybody. After careful study and input from
yourself, Moses, and others, I realized that I purchased this sword
as a piece to study. It has served it's purpose and I personally
love this sword. It is in excellant polish, the Koshirae is better
than average and I will put it up on my web site as soon as I am
able to take good pictures of it. My write up will include something
about the missing Boshi. During the Sengoku Wars, if a tip was
broken off for whatever reason, repair the tip (make a new Kissaki)
and send the Samurai back to fight another day.

All is well............Thanks again,

Elliott
