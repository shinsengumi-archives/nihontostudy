tsuba questions

by davidzincavage

http://zincavage.org/OniandSnail.html


I just bought a tsuba which is quite old and much worn down. On the first
side, it has an oni sitting happily beneath a flowering tree by the light of
a full moon, while below him a snail crawls by. Moon, blossoms, and the
oni's horns are gold. On the reverse, the oni is disappearing away from us,
as the snail crosses the bottom in his direction.

Are tsubas this worn considered to be severely diminished in value?

Is anyone acquainted with a Japanese oni-and-snail story?

Re: [nihontostudy] tsuba questions

by yamaimojg

David,
I think that the surface of the guard was made more or less as you see it. I don't think that it is so old or worn down. It is done in Nara style, but is probably later shiiremono given the quality of the plate and inlay. I don't see oni horns on the front... they look more like the tops of pagoda done in Kaneie-style landscape. The "blossoms" are probably just the usual depiction of pine needle whorls. But, you have the plate in hand and photos are difficult to work with.

Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] tsuba questions

by gaigin2000

Hi David;

While I agree with Jim it is done in Nara style I am not so sure it is a later copy. It may be an original Nara piece done in a more subtle style or a later Nara school work.

From your pictures it is very difficult to tell the color or quality of the iron plate which is the determining factor in iron tsuba and its quality.

The flower and other work seem to be of the Nara school work even if they are hard to see. I would think if someone would go to the trouble to make a copy of a Nara tsuba they would make it a little more flamboyant or copy one of the Nara masters.

John
