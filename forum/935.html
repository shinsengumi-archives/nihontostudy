by yamaimojg

Rich,
Overlapping with what you already said... many early examples were not mounted with kashira at all, and others had non-metal ones. There are also the formal Edo period mounts you mention with horn kashira. Not only loss, but damage affected kashira more than fuchi. Kashira were also more likely to be turned into rings, pendants and bracelet links. Fuchi are also easily removed from tsuka and separated from the tied down kashira. That's all I can think of at the moment. Not sure if it's enough to account for the f/k imbalance.
