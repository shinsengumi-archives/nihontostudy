by yamaimojg

David,
I don't think there are different forms... it's a short section of "unsharpened" edge just above the hamachi. It's not unusual with gendaito, but is rather rare in older blades. It is ground away as the blade is polished over time.

Very old and very good saiha swords that are otherwise intact can sometimes get kanteisho.

Hopefully someone more knowledgeable about swords can comment.

Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] Ubuha

by alangbale

Re retempered blades getting papers, there is at least one Juyo saiba blade,
it is signed 'Chikamura Tatematsuru', its existence proved that the famous
Sanjo Munechika blade signed 'Munechika Murakami' was made by his pupil
Chikamura and had the 'mune' added later. 'kami' and 'tatematsuru' being the
same kanji.

Alan
