Re: Cheap but Usefull katana

by nihontochicken

I'm not into tameshigiri, but did buy a Paul Chen Practical katana on
Ebay for $130 including shipping. I felt I got quite a bit for the
money. Though the blade is of mill steel, it shows fine wisps of
hada (sunagashi). I have read articles by guys in the know who
tested some blades, unfortunately don't remember where, so you'll
have to do a web search to dig them out. I think I remember the Chen
and the United Cutlery swords got good marks in the cheapie
category. For sure, stay away from the cheap stainless steel blades;
they can be brittle and fracture on impact, launching the free end to
who-knows-where. The drawbacks that I can relate re the Chen
Practical Katana include the following. 1. The tsuka is non-
removable (in fact, the nakago has a kink in it to help insure it
stays inside the tsuka); the tsuka must be destroyed to get it off
the blade, and any replacement tsuka may not be removed by just
sliding it off, due to the nakago kink. 2. The shape of the blade is
actually fairly good in that it is reasonably appropriate, regular
and lacking in waves/ripples; however, it is "flat polished", i.e.,
little or no niku (clamshell curve from the shinogi to the ha).
Thus, it should cut rather well, but be more susceptible to chipping
than it would if more traditionally shaped. Even with these
reservations, I feel the Chen katana is a good deal for the money,
particularly if you can get a good discount on Ebay. FWIW.
