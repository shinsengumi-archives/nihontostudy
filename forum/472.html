Re: [nihontostudy] Botan and shishi

by kanjincho

>I have seen the botan (peony) paired many times with a shishi on
>tsuba and fuchi/kashira.
>
>I was wondering, what is the connection between the two/what is the
>theme?


The shishi is considered the king of all animals, and the botan is
considered the king (if not the queen) of all flowers. The pair symbolizes
the best/strongest. The pairing of lion and peony is a Chinese concept,
and the design is still seen today among Chinese works of art/craft. The
peony was brought to Japan from Tang China as medicine, and the flower
became popular among Heian court nobles. Stylized botan flower became
popular design motif, including the "mon" crest, by the Kamakura period. A
branch of the Genji clan in Settsu Province used a "mon" showing shishi and
botan around that period (ca. 1300), so the pairing has been in Japan for
some time.

The botan/shishi pair was (still is?) also a popular irezumi (tattoo) motif
among the yakuza (gangsters).


Best,
