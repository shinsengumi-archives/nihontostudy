what's on the reverse?

by elliottshogun

Here is a question that I and others need the answer to.

On the back of the NBTHK origami, there are columns with information
about the sword that has been given the 'paper'. What do these columns
contain? Is there an order from right to left as to what each column
contains? I can spend time deciphering but it is easier to know what I
am looking for.

'The Japanese Sword' website shows everything except the reverse side
of the NBTHK paper.

Thanks for any help you can give me.

Elliott

#5040 [2007-05-30 13:27:28]

Re: [nihontostudy] what's on the reverse?

by nihontocraft

Hello Elliott.

The back of an NBTHK paper records the owner ( or person submitting ) at the time of the shinsa. The empty columns are there to record new owners. There is no info about the sword on the back of an NBTHK paper.

Best regards,
Danny
