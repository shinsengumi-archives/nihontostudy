Re: [nihontostudy] tsuba id

by yamaimojg

Darcy,
I'm not sure about that surface. It may have had a rough life or it may be the result of artificial aging. The delaminated blister at the lower right has rather sharp edges.

There are a couple of famous examples of this type with a 5 stupa design preserved in shrines, one of which is Juyo Bunkazai. The execution of this one is more naive. If it's legitimate it would be Muromachi period and would probably be attributed to katchushi. The main point of shinsa would be to find out if it really is legit. Reproduction katchushi and tosho guards were made from at least the Meiji period through the present day.
Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: tsuba id

by yamaimojg

Rich,
I've seen a number of similar "white papers." Mostly they've been for generic, mediocre later sukashi tsuba where there was really no basis for attribution to a group. The origami still attests that the piece is a genuine tsuba, and documents the motif, material, etc. Agreed that it's not a very satisfying outcome, but for some pieces there isn't anything more to tell. The alternative would be to just reject the item.

Your guard is more interesting than those generic sukashi tsuba. Saotome seems possible, but the shinsa team must have seen (or not seen) something that stopped them from giving that attribution. Maybe they didn't like the tomoe. If not Saotome it'd probably get a Shoami attribution today.

I don't think I've seen any recent origami with no attribution. Has anyone?

Jim
