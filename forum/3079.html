Greetings all, I am looking into the mei of a new tsuba. It is signed
?????, which reads Kishû Jû Sadanobu according to Nihonto Koza but is
also read as Kishû Jû Teimei in the book Tsuba Kanshoki by Dr. Torigoye
Kazutaro .

? can be pronounced both as Sada and Tei and ? reads as both Nobu and
Mei, both are correct, so which one is right ?

Sadanobu/Teimei is a tsuba artist from the Kii provence.

by drjohnplissenden

Dear Rich

Haynes refers to this artist as Sadanaga (H 07752.0)and notes as
follows:

'D: ca 1650-1750
A group of artists who all signed Kishu ju Sadanaga. There were at
least four or five generations. They seem to have come from the
early Edo period Hoan school. Some read this name as Teimei.
Furukawa catalogue pl27 #153.'

Regards, John.
