Re: [nihontostudy] Nobuie Tsuba Terminology

by davidzincavage

tetsu-ji = surface of the iron plate

yakite shitate = a surface finish resembling the glaze on pottery. Produced by putting the finished plate back into the furnace and allowing the surface to melt slightly.

ji-mon = a pattern of hammer marks on the surface of the tsuba.

tetkotsu = "bones." Lumps produced as the result of the steel being made of a mixture of irons of differing hardness. A key feature in the eyes of tsuba aficionadoes.

mimi = the edge.



What you want to do is get hold of a copy of:

Alan L. Harvie (editor), Tsuba - An Aesthetic Study by Kazutaro Torigoye and Robert E. Hayes, translated from the Tsuba Geijutsu-Kô of Kazutaro Torigoye, Northern California Japanese Sword Club, N.P., March, 2000.



in which you will find a handy glossary, as well as a course in tsuba fundamentals. It is available via:



http://www.ncjsc.org/
