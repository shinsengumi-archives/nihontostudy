wanted to ask for help in identifying this tsuba:

http://tinyurl.com/n47uw

Alternatively, the images can be seen in my folder at
the Nihonto or Nihontostudy group sites.

Details: Some running lines 'bone' lines in the mimi
and hints of fold seams. The tsuba is nice iron, with

seppa dai than along the mimi.

Opinions as to school?

Thank you,

Re: [nihontostudy] Identifying this tsuba?

by yamaimojg

Curran,
There are some Owari characteristics, but guards like this almost always get attributed to Shoami. The small size, the rounding of the rim, texture of the iron and the style of tekkotsu are all Shoami traits. It looks like a nice guard. It's a common overall design, but I don't think I've seen it with gourds at the top and bottom like that. I wonder if the other figures are cloves.

Jim

Re: [nihontostudy] Identifying this tsuba?

by yamaimojg

Curran,
I don't think that there's a better label out there, but hopefully someone will weigh in with one. Be aware that there are guards that show admixtures of style, for example Kyo-Owari blend. Those 50/50 mixes can be frustrating, but I don't know that we'll ever unravel them short of some extensive metallurgical survey.

Personally, I don't see Higo or Umetada in it. Usually Kyo-Shoami is used to describe guards done in marubori and/or nunome zogan. A straight iron sukashi guard would normally be described as Shoami, or if pre-Edo period as Ko-Shoami. Don't be discouraged by the Shoami tag. Good Shoami guards will have nice activity in the rim.

The guard is what it is independent of the label.

Jim
