by soshuju

Nice piece-
It is unfortunately upside down. You are looking at two stupa, stone
"towers", often used as grave markers. The five pieces represent the
five elements and the five realms and I would have to go to my
references for further info. This looks early to my eye but there are
earlier on which this was clearly based. I feel certain though that
these are the stone towers and not the wooden grave markers which take
a similar but thinner and even more elongated form...
-t

Yes, it's bunka shiryo tosogu. The 3rd-6th kanji in that column read bunka shiryo (after migi wo).

The 1st col. to the left of "kanteisho" says henbana gata tsuba mumei Akao. The next col. to the left (smaller writing) is mokko gata tetsu ji sukashibori. Next (in parenthesis) is as you said Edo jidai.

Henbana is literally strange flower. I don't find a actual plant with that name, does anyone know of one? If not, then I guess it means what it says, strange flower shape.

Re: [nihontostudy] Help with Kanteisho

by soshuju

1. Ichi, Henka-gata sukashi tsuba mumei Akao
(item, unsigned flower-shaped Akao tsuba)
2. Mokko-gata, tetsu-ji, sukashi-bori
(four-lobed[quince shaped], iron plate, pierced carving)
3. Edo jidai
(nuff said)
4. Migi wo Bunka Shiryo Tosogu to nintei suru
(The item listed at the right, is hereby recognized as Bunka Shiryo
Tosogu)
5. Heisei ju nen ni gatsu ni-ju hachi nichi
(February, 28th 1998)
6.Nihon Tosogu Bijutsukan, kancho Ikeda Suematsu
("signed" Director of the Nihon Tosogu Bijutsukan, Ikeda Suematsu)

Surfing the net a bit, we found that in Botany a natural mutation in a
flower is known as Henka. Morning Glories (Asagao) often exhibit
natural mutations and are called Henka-Asagao. Henge is another reading
and can mean goblin or ghost. So you could go with "strange-flower"
tsuba, or "Bugbear-tsuba" but I like "Mutant-tsuba!"
-t
