Miike school smith Sada Hide

by dougblain

I have gone through all my books and online resources looking for more
information on a smith of the Chikugo Miike school, Sada Hide. The only
reference I have found is from the Sho-Shin website where the smith is
listed as Sada Hide Bun-Ei 1264. He is not listed in Hawley, Koto
Nihonto Zuikan, JSS cross-index or Yamanka's newsletters. I have a well
worn wakishasi of his with all the typical Miike characteristics (wide
hi, narrow suguha, etc.) and have been trying to find out any
additional information on this particular smith. The signature on the
blade is Miike ju Sada Hide (very faint). Hopefully there are some
better libraries out there...

RE: [nihontostudy] Re: Miike school smith Sada Hide

by dougblain

It is shinogi zukuri, 22 long from the tip to the mune machi, kaki-toshi
and ubu as far as I can tell. The total length is 27.2. Originally it was
very wide since the nakago is 5/16 at the widest. The blade has been
through many polishes and is now narrows to 3/16 (sorry but I will probably
never convert to metric). What is visible of the hamon is very narrow suguha
(1/8) and no turnback on the boshi. The size and length did bother me since
I also thought the wakisashi was introduced later, but it is what it is,
hence my curiosity about any additional information on the smith.

by soshuju

Doug -
The Meikan lists 8 koto smiths that signed Sada (tei) Hide (aki) two
of which are listed as Miike smiths. The one from Bunei is said to be
in the line of Miike Denta and it suggests more info on this smith
may be found in the Kokon Kenko Meizukushi and the Kokon Kaji Mei
Shuroku. The other is an Oei era smith working in Chikugo but only a
niji-mei is given in the listing, more info on this smith is said to
be found in the Kokon Ruiji Meizukushi and the Meizukushi Hiden Sho.
Several of the Sadahide are working in Hirado, Fukunaga points out
that these are considered to be smiths from the Sa school but at
least one theory has it that they originated from Miike...
-t
PS the references mentioned are all old rare books none of which are
in my meagre library.

Re: Miike school smith Sada Hide

by dwmac2000

Doug, There is an excellent article written by Dr.Takeuchi
concerning history of the term Wakizashi. Simply Google "
Historical Classification of Wakizashi." I'm sure you will find it
informative. I also recall reading other material which indicated
swords forged of the length between 1 and 2 shaku from the (approx.)
time period of 1200 to 1400 AD are "very few" in number. After 1400
AD there were many. The "percentage probability" would most likely
place your sword in late Ko-to, perhaps early Shin-to, however, you
can't rule out the possibility of it being a 13/14th century blade.
The faint inscription, many polishes, could place it among the very
few.... Good luck, Dave
