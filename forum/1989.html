by yamaimojg

Milt,
There are about as many soft metal Mito tsuba as there are with iron plate. My thinking Mito was based on the "baroque" handling of the rim and the carving style of the dragon. I did assume that the plate was iron, although now that you mention it I can pick out copper-colored highlights where the patina is thin. The copper plate doesn't change my attribution, but as I said, no doubt there were other groups who could produce the same.

BTW Rich, there was a lot of Mito work in Nara/Hamano style. The best makers had very good technique and if anything their versions are a bit over the top compared to the originals. The carving style of the dragon would be rather unusual for Shoami. The scales are done like Goto dragons, but it's a more flamboyant machi-bori style.
Jim
