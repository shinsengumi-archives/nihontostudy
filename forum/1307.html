Re: [nihontostudy] Info on a Umetada School Tsuba

by yamaimojg

Mark,
Thanks for posting. The Umetada produced a wide range of work. Most books and major collections tend to focus on the famous style of Myoju and other early workers. A lot of that work of course does not have any sukashi decoration. Some do have small areas that are cut out, but I cannot recall any early work that is similar to yours.

I've heard a comment along the lines of "everywhere around the country where there were Shoami, there were also Umetada competing with them." I think that I heard this from Bob Haynes who I think attributed it to John Yumoto. Whether entirely true or not, there were a lot of later Umetada people working in a variety of popular styles. Also, there are very high quality Umetada and Shoami tsuba from Kyoto that are very similar in style and execution.

Without the signature, I'd probably think that your tsuba was Kyo Shoami. It's not too hard to believe that there was some individual or workshop associated with the Umetada group that was making a similar style and sometimes signing them. I'm not aware of any research on this particular type of guard by an Umetada group, or on the two character signature like that. Keep looking through the books and see if you can find anything similar with a longer signature. It's possible that the signature was added later, but I'm not sure that an Umetada nijimei would bring any more prestige than adding a gimei Shoami.

Jim
