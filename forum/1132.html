Even given that the nakago was changed, the shape of the blade is unusual and the shape of that habaki is also pretty odd.

The knife style is called Bade-Bade or Battig or Roentjau or I have
seen it written roentgon or something close to that. It is a Malayan
knife. Sometimes they are single and sometimes double edged. They
can be found in the Celebes, Acheen, West Borneo, Sumatra and I am
sure Indonesia. I am certain that this particular blade is a
Japanese blade that was modified to fit into this style of mounting.
They Malay are a seafaring people who did business with virtually
everyone in SE Asia and the Pacific Isles. It is entirely possible
the blade was traded for, bought or even stolen as pirate booty. It
is an interesting amalgamation of styles! I have never seen one like
this, but I am not surprised considering the trading and pirating
skills of the Malay people!
