Naokatsu swordsmith tsuba

by sworddad

Hi All -

This is kind of a long-shot unless one of you are interested in shinshin-to smith Jirotaro Naokatsu's work with tsuba. These appear on the market from time to time, some examples more interesting than others.

Examples of how they are signed seem to range from "Jirotaro Naokatsu" to niji-mei "Naokatsu" to "Fujiwara Naokatsu." Does anyone have any research available if any of these mei-forms indicate the level of his works? Of course what "seems" somewhat logical, his "signed long" tsuba might indicate his better works, but then that's just my personal guess-work.
