Apologies for the delay, but I didn't want to comment on an auction in progress.
It was quite enough, I thought, to give a heads up to anyone contemplating the
Lot in question. In the event it brought 24,000 English pounds...

I give credit here to a friend of mine who presented me with the image of Lot
180 in the present auction and asked "Do you see anything wrong with this?"
Mmmmm, I didn't. Then he brought out his 1991 Sotheby's catalogue. In 1991 the
tanto tsuka was bound with ito over (I think) white same, and the "ring menuki"
was placed centrally in the tsuka. In the present auction the tsuka is unbound
with a black-lacquered same, and the "ring menuki" (it has a special name I
can't recall) is placed further toward the kashira. My friend regarded this as
a poor quality repair because it did not faithfully replicate the original form
as the kashira is of the type with "eyelets" for tsukaito to go through, and not
the type for an unbound tsuka. My friend is very, very experienced in koshirae
and lacquer repair and meticulous in being faithful to the original form
wherever possible. His hilt binding is of such quality with silk tsukaito that
I wouldn't send a tsuka to Japan for binding. His lacquer repairs have to be
seen before and after to be believed....

So, Gentle People, that's all there is to it.

Best regards,

Barry Thomas
(Melbourne, Australia)
