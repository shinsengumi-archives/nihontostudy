Cleaning and repairing shakudo

by sworddad

Hi all -

A recently acquired kozuka/kogai which seems to have good potential to something mainline Goto--depicting "snow covered mandarin orange tree"--has some damage to it. Specifically, the "blade" of the kogai has several not "nicks" to its edge which are sad to see but I would guess, repairable. Have any of you had repairs done on shakudo kodogu like this before?

The kozuka has a couple spots of patina damage (fingerprinting?) on its backside which would seem, could be gently fixed; the patina blended in, let's say. Here my question refers to the nanako. It has what I first considered to be simply "wear" to the nanako. Looking closer, I suspect the nanako has become smooth in places not from wear, but from clogging caused by oily dirt. I'm curious if any of you have found a "safe" way to remove this oily dirt buildup.

Ron
STL

Ron,

I had this same problem on a Kozuka. Seems that with
age gunk gets in all the textures of them. I'm not
sure that I won't get flamed for this but I found that
a wooden toothpick to pick out the big stuff and then
a very soft-headed toothbrush and some water helps to
break up and get rid of the rest of it. I'm not sure
if this is a good or sanctioned way to do it but it
worked for me.

Bob R

Re: [nihontostudy] Cleaning and repairing shakudo

by yamaimojg

One caution is that cleaning out the nanako on Ko-Goto, Ko-Kinko, etc., is somewhat like cleaning the inner walls of iron sukashi tsuba or the backsides of menuki. Old fittings are old, and cleaning them to the point of making them look new again is not the best thing to do. Not to say that dirt and grime are the goal, but exercise restraint in cleaning early pieces.

Jim
