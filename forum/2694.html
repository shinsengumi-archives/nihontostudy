Re: [nihontostudy] Mr Han Bing Siong

by Barry Thomas

Ah, I am in a deep well of sadness. In my early days of collecting and
intermittently over many years Mr Han and I enjoyed a lively correspondence. He
was unfailingly helpful, even to an excess of research on my behalf. Clearly,
he enjoyed the task because it increased his own knowledge and awareness of
Nihont么. One only has to read his "Study at the Sword Museum" series of
articles in the English NBTHK journals to see the depth of his knowledge and
committment to Nihont么 studies and preservation. His superior knowledge was
invaluable to me as I set about trying to find good swords as a beginner.
Thanks to his influence and stirling scholarly example, and that of many others
in our little community, I have come to regard it as a sacred duty to pass on
such freely given knowledge as I've managed to gain over the years.

Over the years Mr Han was a prolific author, although I haven't seen an article
by him for quite some years now. His writings were thorough pieces of research
eruditely expressed and meticulously footnoted.

Mr Han was a 9th (?) generation Chinese teaching in Indonesia before he left to
live in The Netherlands. He was a lawyer, I believe. I still have a photo of a
kenukigata tachi with a gendait么 he acquired from one of his students, the son
of one of President Soekarno's close aides. Mr Han's wife died some two or so
years ago and I remember her passing being mentioned here. He and his wife had
no children.

I haven't corresponded with Mr Han for some years now, but it is time to go and
have a read through our correspondence and shed a tear for this great man of
Nihont么, in whose shade those such as I still struggle to learn and understand.

Best regards to all,

Barry Thomas.
