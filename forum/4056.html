2 new Tsuba to ask about / Kantei

by neobliviscar

List,

I have 2 tsuba that I would like opinions about.

Tsuba #1: Shishi

http://ph.groups.yahoo.com/group/nihontostudy/photos/view/ff0b?b=8

Tsuba #2: Unknown

http://ph.groups.yahoo.com/group/nihontostudy/photos/view/ff0b?b=9
_____________________________________________________

Both photos are in my YahooGroups folder. Please
ignore the spots of high red. I'm still experimenting
with photography and did not clean these images at
all.

The shishi tsuba is nice. The nanako is very good and
interesting in that it changes pattern on the sections
with the shi-shi. My hesitation is that the general
work is very good but not Goto grade. How else might
this one be attributed?

The second tsuba is solid shakudo of excellent color
and very heavy. The carving is lush and extremely
detailed. Despite the thickness of the piece and the
carving, everything has a smooth roundedness to it so
that there are no obtruding edges.

Any help appreciated. I'd like to figure these out as
much as possible before I consider whether to submit
to shinsa.

Thanks,

Curran

CC,
I like Waki-goto for the shishi mid to late Edo, unusual

I also missed part of the description (thinking these were iron plate) but kinko, well that makes a big difference. No. 8 does make me think Omori work. Re: [nihontostudy] Re: 2 new Tsuba to ask about / Kantei

by neobliviscar

Alan, T-, Milt, Peter, and John,

Thanks for the replies. Alan's on the Owari Kinko was
the only one that had not crossed my mind yet. I do
not have the Owari book he mentioned, so Pete will
hafta comment on that.

Tsuba #1: Work is definitely better than what I know
as Waki Goto. But I came across pieces that reminded
me of it. They were described as "'Kaga Waki Goto".

Tsuba #2: This is the better tsuba of the two. The
sakudo is a beautiful ink color and the piece is
rather heavy. The condition is extremely good. The
shakudo is all a high polish luster. Not nanako. The
quality of the work does remind me of the better Kaga
I have seen. The gold inlay is 99% there, but missing
one tiny spot that helps to reveal that the channels
and the gold inlay are rather deep.
This is the one that has me stumped the most.
Somehow doesn't feel like Kaga work. The shape? The
execution of some of the curves & lines?
I definitely have a lot to learn.

Thank you for the help. If someone can expand on Owari
kinko, I'd like to know more about this work.

Curran
C^3
