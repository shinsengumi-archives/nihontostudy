Nie-no-kui-sagari

by sworddad

Hi all -

The latest Token Bijutsu journal (NBTHK) shows a wonderful tanto by Awataguchi Yoshimitsu (late Kamakura), illustrated on the fold-out page. The boshi of this tanto shows (as described), "...the boshi is sugu then turns back in ko-maru with hakikake then nie-no-kui-sagari (descending nie line)...) Personally, I've never noticed this odd characteristic on any illustrated swords before although it seems "somebody" recently asked me about this kind of thing.

For those of you who do not have this journal, picture the boshi as described and at the end of the rather extended kaeri, there is a short "wisp" of nie that comes down off the end of the kaeri at about a 45 degree angle, into the ji. Interesting odd nie formation! Can anyone elaborate on this characteristic, if it is unique to Yoshimitsu or perhaps Awataguchi blades in general, whatever??? Comments?

Ron
