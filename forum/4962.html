I would think it more likely that the habaki is an expression of the owner/user's political allegiance, rather than indicating a "solid" Tokugawa connection. Of course, by this time I imagine the aoi mon was being used as a "decorator" item by all and sundry with no fear of losing one's head!! As Peter observed, if the habaki is a high quality item it points more to my first suggestion and if poorer quality then to the second. Here in Australia there is a Koto wakizashi by (I think) IESUKE (nijimei) that has a solid gold niju habaki with a sukashi Oda mon. The quality is just excellent, but is it a "connection/allegiance" piece or a "decorator"?? Without a large number of documented similar pieces we will never know.
