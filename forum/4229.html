Menuki Fabrication Question

by klepet33319

Good day all. I was looking at examples of Goto menuki this morning
and I am curious if anyone knows how these were fabricated with
different metals contituting the design? I can see how gold could be
overlaid, but how would they construct something this small with
shakudo, silver and gold parts? Thanks!
Pete Klein

Re: [nihontostudy] Menuki Fabrication Question

by sworddad

Hello Pete -

Not sure if anyone replied to this email or not -- most nihontostudy emails somehow go to my spam file for some reason -- but I think your question is a good one. As one who has always admired quality menuki, I can only recall one small article that appeared in NBTHK's Token Bijutsu many years ago that showed a fellow making menuki, Goto style. That was perhaps 15 or more years ago and I've yet to find anyone who has ever written anything (in any detail) about the making of menuki. I'm not even sure if today's artists in Japan even make menuki. If anyone has knowledge of these techniques, it would be a wonderful service to us if an extensive article -- not just a fluff article -- could be written on the subject for publication on the various styles of making menuki and their decoration. (The making of fuchi/kashira is another neglected subject!)

Ron
STL

Re: Menuki Fabrication Question

by klepet33319

Thanks Gordon and Ron. Over the weekend I did run across a reference
in 'The Goda Collection' book on the use of mercury evaporation
techniques with gold and silver powder addmixture, along with the use
of soldered inlay (onlay) techniques. It really is quite amazing to
think of what they accomplished all those years ago. Being a dentist
I found it rather amusing that they would heat evaporate the mercury
off leaving the gold or silver all the time thinking of all the fuss
today over silver mercury amalgam fillings. I wouldn't be a bit
surprised if there were more than a few 'mad hatters' back then!
