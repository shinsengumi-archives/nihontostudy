Saotome tsuba

by alchemyst

Sometimes things jump out and bite you after you've had them
awhile. I had this tsuba for a while and finally noticed
that it had a dragon in VERY low relief (almost Hoan style
relief etching). One the reverse is another dragon and a
flaming jewel. I first firgured katchushi, then maybe Umetada;
now I believe (?) it to be Saotome (Momoyama Era)
based on comparison with one in Tsuba Kanshoki (2nd Ed, the
one with the English captions), page 35 bottom pic.

http://gcclearn.gcc.vccs.edu/stein/saotome6.jpg

Any thoughts? Saotome or not? Certainly not a style I normally
have associated with Saotome.

Re: [nihontostudy] Re: Saotome tsuba

by yamaimojg

The combination of the sukashi and the dragon isn't adding up to any group I can think of even if it is carved rather than etched. Not to say it's the same type, but it reminds me a bit of the early type of tsuba with a dragon carved down into a plain plate like the 3rd (last) guard here:
http://home.earthlink.net/~jggilbert/kamakura.htm

Some sources call that kind of guard the beginning of the Kamakura-bori style, others see it as an elaboration of the Tosho or Katchushi style. Both may be correct, in that a one-off experiment could grow into a "school." Or both may be wrong and the pieces were made by someone or a group that had no actual connection with the makers of what look to us as likely pre or post styles.

Alan mentioned Hizen -- was that because of the style of the dragon?

Jim
